Lab Brief — Gradient-pore HA scaffold vs uniform HA scaffold (plan-v1)

Continuous radial 100 → 500 μm pore gradient, phase-pure 3D-printed HA, vs uniform 300 μm pore HA control. Primary endpoint: day-14 PicoGreen DNA on MC3T3-E1 Subclone 4. Threshold: ≥ 25 % higher on the gradient scaffold.

Hypothesis root: hypothesis.md · Plan markdown: plan.md · Plan JSON: plan.json · AR scene: ar.json

Original question

A 3D-printed hydroxyapatite scaffold with a gradient pore size (from 100 μm to 500 μm) will exhibit higher osteoblast proliferation rates compared to a uniform pore structure because it better mimics the natural transition between cortical and cancellous bone.

Refined hypothesis

InterventionDLP-printed phase-pure HA cylinder (6 mm Ø × 4 mm) with a continuous radial pore gradient: 100 μm at the outer surface increasing to 500 μm at the central axis. Method: DLP vat photopolymerization of an HA-loaded photocurable slurry, debinded and sintered to a phase-pure ceramic.
OutcomeOsteoblast proliferation by PicoGreen dsDNA (primary) and alamarBlue (orthogonal), with DAPI confocal radial cross-sections to verify spatial distribution. Cells: MC3T3-E1 Subclone 4 (ATCC CRL-2593, CVCL_5440).
ThresholdDay-14 dsDNA ≥ 1.25× uniform-control dsDNA (one-sided two-sample t-test, α = 0.05, n = 6 per group), conditional on match-on-confound: porosity ±3 pp, mass ±5 %, BET ±10 %, XRD β-TCP < 5 %, ICP-MS Ca/P 1.667 ± 0.05.
MechanismCortical → cancellous trabecular-spacing mimicry; small-pore outer shell for early attachment, large-pore core for nutrient/waste diffusion; avoids the necrotic core (uniform large-pore) and surface-skin (uniform small-pore) failure modes; graded curvature/permeability cues per Yang 2022.
ControlUniform 300 μm HA scaffold matched on porosity, mass, BET surface area, HA chemistry (XRD-verified phase-pure HA, ICP-MS Ca/P), and outer geometry. Same printer, same slurry batch, same debinding + sintering schedule, seeded and cultured identically in parallel.

Literature QC

VERDICT: similar-work-exists

Two close-but-imperfect precedents exist (Daskalakis 2024 PCL/HA three-zone gradient; Conoscenti 2025 PLLA continuous 70→200 μm) plus one closest-pure-CaP precedent (Wang 2024 CDHA gradient). No paper fabricates a continuous radial pure-HA pore gradient at 100 → 500 μm with osteoblast proliferation as the primary endpoint. The hypothesis is plausibly novel.

Literature cautions

Pore-size optimum is genuinely contested across the literature (200–350 μm vs 300–500 μm vs 600 μm vs hierarchical). See gradient-pore-design.
Native rabbit-femur Haversian/Volkmann canal diameters are 40–200 μm — the 500 μm core is closer to trabecular spacing than cortical canal diameter. See Zhao 2026.
Karageorgiou & Kaplan 2005 (canonical "100 μm minimum, ~300 μm optimal") is non-OA and not in raw/; cited transitively only. See paywalled-or-non-OA-canonical-source-not-captured.
At least one HA-scaffold + osteogenesis claim was retracted in 2025 for image reuse; cross-validate every imaging-based readout. See image-reuse-unsupported-osteogenesis-claims.

Protocol

1

Generate parametric CAD: gradient and uniform-pore scaffolds

4 h

The hypothesis specifies a continuous 100 → 500 μm radial gradient. Two cylindrical 6 mm Ø × 4 mm scaffolds are designed: (a) gradient — pore-size field d(r) = 100 μm at r = 3 mm increasing linearly to 500 μm at r = 0; (b) uniform — pore size 300 μm throughout. Both matched on total porosity (target 65 ± 3 pp) and outer geometry. The 4 mm height puts the diffusion-limited core threshold inside the scaffold so the gradient's diffusion-mitigation claim is testable. TPMS gyroid topology is preferred for its CAD-to-physical fidelity.

Failure warnings

medium cad-vs-actual-pore-size-mismatch
Treat the CAD pore-size axis as a target, not the truth. Build a parametric model that allows S = d_post/d_CAD compensation per pore-bin from Step 2's calibration; do not lock final geometry until Step 2 produces a measured shrinkage lookup.
medium abrupt-interface-stress-concentration
Use a continuous radial pore-size field (voxel-by-voxel), not a discrete-zone/biphasic design — continuous gradients avoid stress concentrators at zone boundaries.

2

Print + sinter calibration array; build CAD-to-measured shrinkage lookup

24 h

Every claim of "100 → 500 μm gradient" fails without μCT verification: AM resolution + slurry rheology + sintering shrinkage compound to shift pores 15–25 % and obliterate features below ~80 μm. Print and sinter a 6×6 array with CAD pores 50 / 100 / 150 / 200 / 300 / 500 / 700 μm; μCT post-sinter; compute S = d_post/d_CAD per pore bin; build a lookup so the experimental CAD is pre-compensated (e.g., 100 μm target → ~125 μm CAD if S ≈ 0.80). Bose 2017 cites 60–70 vol % HA loading minimises shrinkage. The output is a per-batch lookup that defines the actual experimental geometry for Step 3.

Reagents

  • hydroxyapatite · 60 vol % slurry loading · Plasma Biotal · P88 / Captal
  • photoinitiator-TPO · 1 wt % of resin phase · Sigma-Aldrich · 415952

Failure warnings

high sintering-shrinkage-pore-deviation
Pre-compensate CAD by S = d_post/d_CAD ≈ 0.80; calibrate per powder lot, slurry batch, sintering schedule. 6×6 array spans 50–700 μm CAD pores.
high HA-phase-decomposition-during-sintering
Cap sintering at 1200–1250 °C; verify Ca/P 1.667 ± 0.05 by ICP-MS; XRD post-sinter to confirm β-TCP fraction < 5 %.
high cytotoxic-residue-from-binders-or-photoinitiators
Slow 0.5 °C/min debinding through 300–600 °C with ≥ 2 h soak; EtOH/water reflux leach; ISO-10993-5 cytotoxicity gate at Step 5.
medium cad-vs-actual-pore-size-mismatch
Pair μCT (≤ 10 μm voxel) with SEM cross-sections; mercury intrusion porosimetry on a sister scaffold for an independent pore-throat distribution.

4

Characterise each batch: μCT, XRD, BET, ICP-MS, mechanical

48 h (parallel)

Match-on-confound parameters must be verified, not assumed. μCT every experimental scaffold (≤ 10 μm voxel); thresholded pore-size histogram per radial bin (outer 0–500 μm, mid, core). XRD on one scaffold per batch (Cu-Kα, 2θ = 20–60°, 0.02° step, 2 s dwell, Rietveld); BET N₂ adsorption surface area; ICP-MS Ca/P; mercury intrusion on a sister scaffold; SEM-EDX cross-sections; mechanical compression on 3 sister scaffolds per group.

Acceptance gate: gradient and uniform groups within ±3 pp porosity, ±10 % BET, β-TCP < 5 %, Ca/P 1.667 ± 0.05, mass within ±5 %.

Failure warnings

high cad-vs-actual-pore-size-mismatch
Reject any scaffold whose measured outer-zone pore mean falls outside 100 ± 25 μm or core mean outside 500 ± 50 μm.
high HA-phase-decomposition-during-sintering
XRD per batch; reject batch if β-TCP fraction > 5 % by Rietveld.
medium HA-stoichiometry-affects-resorption
ICP-MS Ca/P on acid-digested scaffold (one per batch); confirm 1.667 ± 0.05.

5

Sterilise scaffolds; ISO-10993-5 cytotoxicity pre-screen on L929

7

Pre-coat scaffolds, dynamic-perfusion seed at 5 × 10⁴ MC3T3 per scaffold

8 h

Static seeding loses 30–60 % of cells through 500 μm pores and biases the readout in favour of small-pore scaffolds — falsely inflating the gradient effect. Pre-coat with 10 μg/mL fibronectin × 1 h at 37 °C; mount each scaffold in custom perfusion chamber; oscillating flow at 0.5 mL/min × 4 h; recover outlet medium and count by hemocytometer to compute per-scaffold seeding efficiency. Reject any scaffold where seeded count differs from group mean by > 2 SD. Conoscenti 2025 reported 22.8 ± 1.3 % efficiency uniformly across a gradient.

Reagents

  • PBS · wash buffer · Gibco · 10010-023
  • alpha-MEM + 10 % FBS + 1 % P/S · seeding medium

Validation

Primary endpoint: day-14 dsDNA per scaffold (PicoGreen, lysate, blank-subtracted) — gradient vs uniform 300 μm control.

Success criteria

  • Gradient d14 dsDNA ≥ 1.25× uniform-control d14 dsDNA (one-sided two-sample t-test, α = 0.05, n = 6 per group).
  • alamarBlue d14 fluorescence directionally consistent; (alamarBlue / PicoGreen) ratio within ±25 % between groups (both groups remained proliferative).
  • Scaffold groups pass match-on-confound spec: porosity ±3 pp, BET ±10 %, β-TCP < 5 %, Ca/P 1.667 ± 0.05, mass ±5 %.
  • Seeding efficiency between groups within ±20 %, no group below 60 % (perfusion).
  • L929 ISO-10993-5 viability ≥ 80 % on conditioned medium pre-MC3T3 release.
  • DAPI cross-sections show gradient-core cell density ≥ 50 % of outer-zone density (confirms diffusion-mitigation).

Failure criteria

  • Gradient d14 dsDNA < 1.25× uniform-control AND/OR p > 0.05 → hypothesis falsified.
  • Match-on-confound spec violated → result inadmissible (gradient effect not isolable from chemistry/porosity confound).
  • Seeding efficiency between groups > ±20 % OR DAPI hollow large-pore core in gradient → re-design seeding (longer perfusion, fibronectin) before re-running.
  • L929 viability < 80 % → re-process scaffolds; do NOT seed MC3T3.
  • alamarBlue / PicoGreen ratio drifts > 50 % by d14 → cells differentiated unintentionally; revisit medium / passage.
  • Image-audit discrepancy → full re-audit before any reporting.
