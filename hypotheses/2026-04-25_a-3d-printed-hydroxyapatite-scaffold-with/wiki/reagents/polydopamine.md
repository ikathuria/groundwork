# Polydopamine (PDA)

Mussel-inspired adhesive coating formed by oxidative polymerisation of dopamine in mildly basic Tris buffer. Coats virtually any surface (including HA) with a thin (~10–50 nm) layer presenting catechol and amine groups for further functionalisation (drug binding, antibody / peptide immobilisation).

## Properties

- **Synthesis:** 2 mg/mL dopamine HCl in 10 mM Tris pH 8.5, overnight at room temperature.
- **Coating thickness:** ~10–50 nm depending on time.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Surface-functionalising HA / CDHA / PCL scaffolds for drug or growth-factor immobilisation.
- Improving cell adhesion through catechol-Ca²⁺ chemistry on HA.

## Suppliers / catalog

In-house from dopamine HCl (Sigma H8502 or similar).

## Alternatives

- Plasma treatment, silane chemistry — alternative surface activation.
- Direct adsorption + crosslinker (EDC/NHS).

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- pH sensitive — Tris buffer pH 8.5 is the typical sweet spot.
- Coating colour and thickness depend on O₂ availability; cap loosely / shake.
