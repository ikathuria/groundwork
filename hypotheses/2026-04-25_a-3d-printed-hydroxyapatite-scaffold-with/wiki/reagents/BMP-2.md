# Bone morphogenetic protein 2 (BMP-2)

The most potent osteoinductive growth factor in clinical use. Recombinant human BMP-2 (dibotermin alfa) is FDA-approved as INFUSE for spinal fusion. In scaffold studies, BMP-2 is loaded onto HA scaffolds (adsorption, covalent immobilisation, or via PLGA microsphere release) to drive in-vivo bone formation.

## Properties

- **MW:** ~26 kDa (homodimer)
- **Pathway:** binds BMPR-IA / BMPR-II → SMAD1/5/8 → RUNX2 → osteogenic gene programme.
- **Working concentration in vitro:** 50–200 ng/mL.
- **In-vivo dose:** clinically high (mg/cm³ scaffold) — much higher than in-vitro because of low retention and rapid clearance.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Loaded on HA / β-TCP / BCP scaffolds for in-vivo bone-defect repair.
- In-vitro osteogenic-induction in MSC and primary osteoblast cultures.
- Dose-response studies for delivery-system optimisation.

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/BMP-7]] (OP-1) — similar pathway, less common.
- [[../reagents/dexamethasone]] — small-molecule pro-osteogenic; cheaper, less potent.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/in-vivo-vs-in-vitro-discordance]] — clinical BMP-2 uses 1000–10,000× higher doses than in-vitro; in-vivo retention is the gating issue, not intrinsic activity.
- Heterotopic / ectopic ossification at high dose; off-target effects.
- Cost: research-grade rhBMP-2 is one of the most expensive cell-culture reagents (~$500/10 µg).
- Charge/pI ~9 → adsorbs strongly to negative surfaces (HA); release kinetics depend on substrate chemistry.
