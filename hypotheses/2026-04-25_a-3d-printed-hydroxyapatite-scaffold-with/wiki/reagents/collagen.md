# Collagen (Type I)

The dominant ECM protein in bone matrix; the organic template on which HA biomineralises in vivo (per [[../sources/2008-palmer-biomimetic-systems-for-hydroxyapatite-mi]]). Used in scaffolds either as the bulk matrix (collagen sponges) or as a coating to humanise synthetic scaffolds.

## Properties

- **CAS:** 9007-34-5
- **Structure:** triple helix of three α-chains (~1000 aa each), ~285 kDa per tropocollagen molecule.
- **Sources:** rat-tail (most common research), bovine, porcine, recombinant human.
- **Self-assembly:** forms fibrils from neutralised acid solution at 37 °C.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Scaffold matrix in collagen-HA composites; collagen-glycosaminoglycan ([[../reagents/collagen-glycosaminoglycan]]) sponges.
- Coating on HA / PCL scaffolds for cell-attachment improvement.
- Biomimetic template for in-situ HA mineralisation.

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/gelatin]] — denatured collagen; simpler handling.
- [[../reagents/silk-fibroin]] — natural protein matrix with higher mechanical strength.
- [[../reagents/chitosan]], [[../reagents/alginate]] — non-mammalian polysaccharide matrices.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- pH-sensitive: gelation requires neutral pH; acidified stock (typical) must be neutralised on ice immediately before use.
- Lot-to-lot variability is high in animal-derived collagen; recombinant human is more reproducible.
- Telopeptide content affects immunogenicity; atelocollagen is preferred for clinical work.
