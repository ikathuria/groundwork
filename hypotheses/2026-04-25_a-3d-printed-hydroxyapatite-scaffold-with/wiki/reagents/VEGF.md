# Vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF)

Pro-angiogenic factor. Loaded into bone scaffolds to support vascular ingrowth — necessary for any large or thick scaffold to avoid the diffusion-limited necrotic core.

## Properties

- **VEGF-165** is the dominant isoform used in scaffold work.
- **Working concentration:** 5–50 ng/mL in vitro.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Co-delivered with BMP-2 in dual-factor scaffolds.
- Coatings to encourage HUVEC attachment / capillary formation.

## Alternatives

- bFGF (FGF-2), PDGF — alternative angiogenic factors.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- Heparin-binding; HA scaffolds with negative surfaces may bind VEGF tightly and reduce release.
- Short half-life in serum-free media; sustained delivery from a depot is the design challenge.
