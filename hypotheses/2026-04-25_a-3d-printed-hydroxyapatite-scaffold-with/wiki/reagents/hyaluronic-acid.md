# Hyaluronic acid (HA-sugar)

Linear glycosaminoglycan; ECM component. Note: in this wiki **"HA"** refers to [[../reagents/hydroxyapatite]] unless otherwise specified — this reagent is consistently called "hyaluronic acid" or "HA-sugar."

## Properties

- **CAS:** 9004-61-9
- **MW:** native ~1–10 MDa; commercial 10 kDa to 2 MDa.
- **Structure:** repeating disaccharide of D-glucuronic acid + N-acetyl-D-glucosamine.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Hydrogel matrix for cell encapsulation.
- Component of cartilage / interface-tissue gradient scaffolds.

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/chondroitin-6-sulphate]] — different GAG.
- [[../reagents/alginate]] — synthetic-feel ionic gel.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- Hygroscopic; highly viscous solutions difficult to handle at high MW.
- MW degradation by hyaluronidase / shear / radiation reduces functional properties.
