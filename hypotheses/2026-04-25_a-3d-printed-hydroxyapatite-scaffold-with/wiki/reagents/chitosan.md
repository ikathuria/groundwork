# Chitosan

Linear polysaccharide derived from deacetylation of chitin (crustacean shells). Cationic, biocompatible, antimicrobial, biodegradable. Used as scaffold matrix and as drug-delivery carrier; appears in HA composites and gradient scaffolds.

## Properties

- **CAS:** 9012-76-4
- **Source:** chitin from shrimp/crab shells (vegan alternative from fungi available).
- **Degree of deacetylation:** typically 75–95%; affects solubility and degradation.
- **Solubility:** dilute acid (acetic, lactic); precipitates at neutral pH.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Matrix or coating in HA-chitosan composite scaffolds.
- Hydrogel for cell encapsulation.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- pH-sensitive: dissolves only in acid; gelation by pH neutralisation. Cell-toxicity from residual acid if not buffered.
- Wide MW / DD variation between lots.
