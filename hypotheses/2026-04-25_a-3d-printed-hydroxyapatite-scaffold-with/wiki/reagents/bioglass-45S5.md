# 45S5 Bioglass

The original Hench bioactive glass (1969). Forms a hydroxycarbonate apatite (HCA) layer on contact with body fluids, bonding directly to bone. Out-of-scope as a primary scaffold for the HA-gradient hypothesis but appears throughout the literature as a comparator.

## Properties

- **Composition (wt%):** 45 SiO₂ / 24.5 Na₂O / 24.5 CaO / 6 P₂O₅
- **Tg:** ~550 °C; Tc: ~700 °C
- **Bioactivity:** Class A — forms HCA in vitro within hours in SBF.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Out-of-scope as primary scaffold per hypothesis but cited as comparator in many of the broad scaffold reviews.
- Frit / particulate filler in composite scaffolds.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/brittle-ceramic-mechanical-failure]] — 45S5 has very low fracture toughness; scaffolds prone to micro-crack propagation.
- [[../failure-modes/sintering-shrinkage-pore-deviation]] — 45S5 crystallises during sintering; window between densification and crystallisation is narrow.
