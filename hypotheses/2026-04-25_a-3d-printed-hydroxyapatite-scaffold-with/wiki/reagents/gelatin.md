# Gelatin

Denatured collagen; loses triple-helix structure during extraction. Cheaper and easier to handle than native collagen; the basis of GelMA (methacrylated gelatin) bioinks for DLP printing.

## Properties

- **CAS:** 9000-70-8
- **Type A** (acid-extracted, porcine) vs **Type B** (alkali-extracted, bovine) — pI ~9 vs ~5.
- **Bloom number:** strength rating, typically 175–300.
- **Sol-gel transition:** ~30 °C in water.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Bioink base for DLP / extrusion printing (often as GelMA).
- Sacrificial porogen in scaffold fabrication.
- Cell-attachment-promoting coating.

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/collagen]] — native triple-helix; more bioactive.
- [[../reagents/alginate]], [[../reagents/chitosan]], [[../reagents/silk-fibroin]] — natural polymer alternatives.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- Sol-gel transition near body temperature → unstable scaffolds without crosslinking.
- GelMA requires photoinitiator and UV/visible light; residual methacrylate is a cytotoxicity concern.
