# Transforming growth factor β (TGF-β)

Family of pleiotropic cytokines (TGF-β1, β2, β3). In bone TE used for chondrogenic and pre-osteogenic priming; in interface tissues (bone-cartilage gradient) used to direct chondrogenic ends of zonal cultures.

## Properties

- **MW:** ~25 kDa homodimer
- **Working concentration:** 1–10 ng/mL.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Chondrogenic differentiation in osteochondral / interface gradient scaffolds.
- Component of MSC-priming cocktails.

## Alternatives

- BMP family for purely osteogenic.
- IGF-1 for proliferation-biased priming.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- Latent vs active forms — recombinant is typically active mature; serum contains latent.
- Sticks to plastic; carrier protein (BSA) recommended in dilute solutions.
