# Strontium (Sr)

Calcium-mimicking divalent cation. Substitutes into HA lattice (Sr-substituted HA, Sr-CaP) at up to ~10 mol%; reportedly pro-osteogenic + anti-osteoclastic. Common dopant in modern HA / β-TCP scaffolds.

## Properties

- **Atomic number:** 38; ionic radius (1.18 Å) close to Ca²⁺ (1.00 Å).
- **Substitution into HA lattice** confirmed up to ~10 mol% without major phase change.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Dopant in HA / β-TCP scaffolds for enhanced osteogenesis.
- Sr-substituted CaP coatings.

## Alternatives

- Mg, Zn, Cu, Si — other ionic dopants for CaP.
- BMP-2, dex — pharmacological osteogenic stimuli.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/HA-stoichiometry-affects-resorption]] — Sr substitution alters HA lattice and dissolution kinetics; matched-chemistry comparisons must control Sr content.
- Strontium ranelate had cardiovascular safety concerns and was withdrawn from many clinical markets — a translation caution, not directly relevant to local-release scaffolds.
