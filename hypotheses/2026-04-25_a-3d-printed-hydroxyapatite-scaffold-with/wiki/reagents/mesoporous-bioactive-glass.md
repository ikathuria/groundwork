# Mesoporous bioactive glass (MBG)

Sol-gel-derived bioactive glass with engineered 2–50 nm mesopores templated by surfactants. High specific surface area (300–800 m²/g) drives accelerated apatite formation and supports drug-loading.

## Properties

- **Composition:** typically 80 SiO₂ / 15 CaO / 5 P₂O₅ (mol%) or ternary variants.
- **Surface area:** 300–800 m²/g (vs ~50 m²/g for melt-quenched 45S5).
- **Pore size:** 2–10 nm typical; tunable by template.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- High-surface-area filler for accelerated bioactivity.
- Drug-delivery carrier (loaded into HA scaffolds).

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/bioglass-45S5]] — melt-quenched, lower surface area.
- [[../reagents/hydroxyapatite]] — primary CaP comparator.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/brittle-ceramic-mechanical-failure]] — mesoporous structure further reduces strength.
- Surfactant template removal is critical — residual organics are cytotoxic.
