# Silk fibroin

Structural protein from Bombyx mori silkworm cocoons. Mechanically strongest of the natural polymers; biocompatible; tunable degradation by β-sheet content. Used in HA-silk composite scaffolds.

## Properties

- **CAS:** 9009-39-8
- **Processing:** cocoons degummed (sericin removal) → dissolved in LiBr → dialysed → aqueous solution → spin/cast/print.
- **β-sheet content** controls mechanical properties and water-stability.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Composite matrix in silk-HA scaffolds.
- Coating to humanise synthetic scaffolds.

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/collagen]], [[../reagents/gelatin]], [[../reagents/chitosan]] — natural-polymer alternatives.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- Sericin (the coating protein) is immunogenic; degumming step is critical.
- Lithium-bromide processing residues are cytotoxic — exhaustive dialysis required.
