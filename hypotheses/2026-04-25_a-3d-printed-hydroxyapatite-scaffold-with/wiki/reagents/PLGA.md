# Poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA)

Copolymer of lactic and glycolic acids. Tunable degradation (weeks–months) by varying the L:G ratio; FDA-approved for many implants and drug-delivery vehicles. Common matrix in scaffolds where intermediate degradation between PLA and faster-resorbing materials is needed.

## Properties

- **CAS:** 26780-50-7 (50:50 nominal)
- **Common ratios:** 50:50 (fastest), 65:35, 75:25, 85:15 (slowest); higher lactide → slower degradation.
- **Tg:** 40–60 °C depending on ratio and Mn
- **Solubility:** chloroform, DCM, THF, ethyl acetate; sometimes acetone for low-MW grades.

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- Scaffold matrix in PLGA-HA composites; faster-degrading alternative to PCL.
- Microspheres for sustained release of growth factors (BMP-2, VEGF) loaded into ceramic scaffolds.
- Composite coating to deliver drugs from HA scaffolds.

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/polycaprolactone]] — slower degradation.
- [[../reagents/polylactic-acid]] — slower again, more crystalline.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/cytotoxic-residue-from-binders-or-photoinitiators]] — bulk autocatalytic acidic degradation in thick PLGA scaffolds creates an acidic core that kills cells; pair with HA to buffer.
- Hygroscopic; store ≤ –20 °C desiccated; bring to room temperature before opening to avoid water condensation.
