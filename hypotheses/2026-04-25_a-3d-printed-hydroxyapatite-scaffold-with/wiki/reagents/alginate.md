# Alginate (sodium alginate)

Linear polysaccharide from brown seaweed; forms hydrogels by ionic crosslinking with divalent cations (Ca²⁺, Sr²⁺, Ba²⁺). Common bioink and microcapsule material; in HA scaffolds used as a sacrificial template or as the matrix in alginate-HA composites.

## Properties

- **CAS:** 9005-38-3
- **Structure:** β-D-mannuronate (M) + α-L-guluronate (G) blocks; M/G ratio determines gel strength.
- **Crosslinking:** ionic, reversible by chelation (EDTA, citrate).

## Common uses in this hypothesis space

- DIW bioink base; calcium-crosslinked post-print.
- Sacrificial template (dissolved out by Ca-chelation).

## Alternatives

- [[../reagents/gelatin]], [[../reagents/collagen]], [[../reagents/chitosan]] — natural polymer hydrogels.
- [[../reagents/PEG-DA]] — synthetic photocurable hydrogel.

## Gotchas / things to watch for

- Endotoxin content varies widely between grades; ultrapure (UP) grades for cell work.
- Ionic crosslink reversible — physiological Na⁺ slowly displaces Ca²⁺, weakening gels.
