# Dynamic / perfusion cell seeding

## What it measures / produces

Cell suspension is perfused through the scaffold under controlled flow (typically 0.1–1 mL/min) using a peristaltic or syringe pump in a custom seeding chamber. Output: more uniform cell distribution through the scaffold thickness than static seeding, with higher overall efficiency (60–80% vs 10–40% for static).

## When to use it

Use perfusion seeding when (a) static seeding gives biased / inadequate cell distribution, (b) the scaffold core must be seeded (long diffusion path, large or thick scaffolds), (c) you can afford the equipment / time cost.

## Key parameters

- **Flow rate**: 0.1–1 mL/min for cell suspensions; higher rates damage cells.
- **Cell density**: similar to static (5 × 10⁴ – 5 × 10⁵ per scaffold).
- **Seeding time**: 30 min – 4 h depending on scaffold thickness.

## Things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/cell-seeding-efficiency-variation]] — perfusion can be more variable across replicates due to flow distribution differences.
- [[../failure-modes/donor-variability-hMSC]] — primary cells differ in attachment kinetics.
