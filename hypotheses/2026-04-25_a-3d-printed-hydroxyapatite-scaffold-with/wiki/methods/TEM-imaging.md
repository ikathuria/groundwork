# Transmission electron microscopy (TEM)

## What it measures / produces

TEM transmits a focused electron beam through a thin (<100 nm) sample; output is high-resolution image with sub-nm resolution, including selected-area electron diffraction (SAED) for crystal-orientation analysis. For HA: visualizes apatite-platelet alignment along collagen fibrils, crystallite size, and lattice imperfections.

## When to use it

Use TEM for sub-nm structural / crystallographic information beyond what SEM resolves. Niche for scaffold work; central for biomineralisation chemistry.
