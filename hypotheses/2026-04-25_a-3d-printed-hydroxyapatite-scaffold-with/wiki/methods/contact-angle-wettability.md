# Contact angle / wettability

## What it measures / produces

Sessile-drop measurement of the contact angle between a water droplet and the scaffold surface. Output: contact angle (°); <90° hydrophilic, >90° hydrophobic. Wettability modulates protein adsorption and cell attachment.

## When to use it

Use contact angle (a) to verify surface treatment (PDA, polydopamine, plasma) effectiveness — Wang et al. measured contact-angle changes with increasing PDA concentration on CDHA scaffolds [[../sources/2024-wang-polydopamine-functionalized-calcium-defi]], (b) to track sintering effects on surface chemistry, (c) to predict cell attachment behavior.

## Step-level notes

- HA itself is moderately hydrophilic (~60–80°).
- Note that **higher hydrophobicity may favour proliferation** via altered fibronectin adsorption (Kennedy et al. cited in [[../sources/2018-zhang-effect-of-microporosity-on-scaffolds-for]]).
