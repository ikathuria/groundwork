# Electrospinning

## What it measures / produces

Electrospinning produces sub-micron polymer fibres by extruding a polymer solution through a needle held at high voltage; the electrostatic field stretches the jet into a fine fibre that lands on a grounded collector. Output: nonwoven fibre mats with fibre diameters 100 nm – 5 μm, high porosity, and high surface area. Used for ECM-mimicking scaffolds, particularly tendon/cartilage and tendon-bone interfaces. Honeycomb-pattern collectors can produce **gradient fibre patterns from 80 to 360 μm pore diameter** [[../sources/2017-gao-bone-biomaterials-and-interactions-with]].

## When to use it

Use electrospinning for fibre/composition-gradient scaffolds (especially tendon-bone interface) [[../sources/2025-xie-recent-advances-in-gradient-biomimetic-s]] [[../sources/2025-liu-gradient-scaffolds-in-bone-soft-tissue-i]]. Less applicable to monolithic HA bone scaffolds because pure ceramic cannot be electrospun (only HA-loaded polymer composites at moderate loading).

## Key parameters

- **Fibre diameter**: 100 nm – 5 μm.
- **Voltage**: 10–30 kV.
- **Polymer solutions**: PCL/PLA/PLGA in chloroform-DMF; silk fibroin in formic acid; collagen / gelatin in HFIP.
- **Honeycomb-collector pore range**: 80–360 μm achievable [[../sources/2017-gao-bone-biomaterials-and-interactions-with]].

## Common variants

- **Conventional electrospinning** — flat or rotating drum collector.
- **Coaxial electrospinning** — see [[coaxial-printing-spinning]].
- **Aligned vs random fibres** — controlled by drum vs flat collector and rotation speed.
- **Gradient-collector electrospinning** — patterned collector creates pore-size gradient [[../sources/2017-gao-bone-biomaterials-and-interactions-with]].

## Things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/pore-anisotropy-from-print-direction]] — fibre orientation gives strong anisotropy.
- [[../failure-modes/brittle-ceramic-mechanical-failure]] — for HA-loaded electrospun composites.
