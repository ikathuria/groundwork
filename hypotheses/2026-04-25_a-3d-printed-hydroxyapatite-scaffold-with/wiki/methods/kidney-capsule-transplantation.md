# Kidney capsule transplantation

## What it measures / produces

Kidney capsule transplantation places a small graft (cells, scaffold, tissue fragment) under the renal capsule of a host mouse — an ectopic, well-vascularised, immunoprivileged site. Output: bone-forming-ability (BFA) of skeletal stem cells or scaffolds, assessed by histology / μCT after 4–8 weeks. Used in [[../sources/protocol-2023-uchida]] for mouse skeletal stem cell BFA + limiting dilution assay.

## When to use it

Niche method, primarily for stem-cell BFA characterization rather than scaffold testing. Less applicable to the present hypothesis directly.

## Things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/in-vivo-vs-in-vitro-discordance]] — ectopic bone formation may not predict orthotopic defect repair.
