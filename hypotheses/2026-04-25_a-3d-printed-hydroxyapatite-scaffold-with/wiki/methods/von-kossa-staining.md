# Von Kossa staining

## What it measures / produces

Von Kossa staining detects phosphate deposits in mineralised matrix: silver nitrate reacts with phosphate to form silver phosphate, which is reduced under UV light to elemental silver, staining mineralised regions black/brown. Orthogonal to alizarin red (which detects Ca²⁺).

## When to use it

Use von Kossa as a phosphate-side mineralisation confirmation alongside alizarin red (calcium-side) for cell-deposited matrix mineralisation. Less common for HA scaffolds because HA itself contains phosphate (similar confound to ARS).

## Things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/ARS-binds-residual-scaffold-HA]] — analogous confound: HA scaffolds contain phosphate; staining is non-specific. Cell-free scaffold blanks essential.
