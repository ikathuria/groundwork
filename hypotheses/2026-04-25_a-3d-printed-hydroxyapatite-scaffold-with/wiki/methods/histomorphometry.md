# Histomorphometry

## What it measures / produces

Quantitative analysis of bone histology sections to extract bone-volume fraction, trabecular geometry, osteoid thickness, and bone-formation rate (with fluorochrome labelling). Output: 2D-section quantification complementary to 3D μCT.

## When to use it

Use histomorphometry for explanted bone-defect samples, paired with μCT. Standard in long-term in-vivo studies.

## Things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/in-vivo-vs-in-vitro-discordance]] — histomorphometry is the in-vivo bridge from in-vitro proliferation.
