# Precision extruding deposition (PED)

## What it measures / produces

Solvent-free 3D printing of thermoplastic (PCL) through a **50 μm-inner-diameter nozzle**, producing fine struts and "grid-in-grid" hierarchical pore architectures. Output: hierarchical multi-scale pore PCL scaffolds with global pores (~400–500 μm strand spacing) and local pores within strands [[../sources/2025-lee-pcl-scaffold-with-well-defined-hierarchi]].

## When to use it

Use PED when you need (a) fine pre-printable thermoplastic features (down to ~50 μm), (b) hierarchical multi-scale pore architectures, (c) cell-alignment via local strand wrinkles. Demonstrated cell-alignment ranking C > A > B > D across four geometric variants [[../sources/2025-lee-pcl-scaffold-with-well-defined-hierarchi]].
