# Phalloidin (F-actin) staining

## What it measures / produces

Phalloidin is a cyclic peptide from Amanita phalloides that binds with high affinity to filamentous (F-) actin; conjugated to fluorescent dyes (Alexa Fluor 488/568/647, rhodamine, FITC) for cytoskeleton imaging. Output: high-contrast images of stress fibres, lamellipodia, focal-adhesion-coupled cytoskeleton — diagnostic for cell spreading and mechanotransduction.

## When to use it

Use phalloidin to assess cell morphology, spreading area, and cytoskeletal organisation on scaffolds — especially when comparing scaffolds expected to differ in mechanotransductive cues. Yang et al. used phalloidin to show that high-Gaussian-curvature TPMS scaffolds increase F-actin stress fibres in hMSCs [[../sources/2022-yang-gaussian-curvature-driven-direction-of-c]].

## Key parameters

- **Working concentration**: 1:40–1:200 dilution of fluorescent-conjugated phalloidin in PBS.
- **Fixation**: 4% paraformaldehyde, 15 min RT.
- **Permeabilisation**: 0.1% Triton X-100, 5 min.
- **Incubation**: 30–60 min at RT, light-protected.

## Things to watch for

- [[../failure-modes/HA-autofluorescence-confounds-livedead]] — porous HA can fluoresce in green/red channels.
