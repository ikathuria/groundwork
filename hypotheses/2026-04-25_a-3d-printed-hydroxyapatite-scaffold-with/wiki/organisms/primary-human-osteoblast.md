# Primary human osteoblast

## What it is

Primary human osteoblasts isolated from surgical bone explants — most commonly femoral-head fragments from total-hip-arthroplasty waste, iliac-crest biopsies, or alveolar / mandibular bone from oral-surgery waste. Isolation is by sequential collagenase digestion (the Robey & Termine protocol family) or by explant outgrowth. The cells are non-immortalized, diploid, and committed to the osteoblast lineage (in contrast to hBMSCs, which are upstream multipotent precursors). Commercial sources include Lonza (CC-2538), PromoCell (C-12720), and ScienCell (4600).

## Growth conditions

- **Medium**: osteoblast growth medium (Lonza OGM; PromoCell C-27001) — proprietary, generally α-MEM or DMEM-based with 10% FBS + ascorbic acid.
- **Atmosphere**: 37 °C, 5% CO₂.
- **Subculturing**: avoid > P4–P6; primary human osteoblasts dedifferentiate quickly toward fibroblast-like phenotype with passaging.
- **Donor metadata**: track donor age, sex, anatomic site, and disease status (osteoporotic vs healthy) — all affect proliferation kinetics.

## Why pick this organism for this hypothesis

- The committed-osteoblast human-primary cell — closest in vitro to "the cell whose proliferation we're trying to enhance".
- Useful as a confirmatory arm if the hypothesis's gradient-vs-uniform effect is to be claimed for clinical osteoblasts (vs hBMSC precursors or MC3T3-E1 mouse cells).
- More expensive and donor-variable than hBMSC; rarely used as the primary screen — usually a confirmatory n=2–3 donor arm.
