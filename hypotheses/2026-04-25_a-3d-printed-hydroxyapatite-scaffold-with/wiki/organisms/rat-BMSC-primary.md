# Rat BMSC (primary)

## What it is

Primary rat bone-marrow-derived MSCs, isolated by flushing rat long-bone marrow (typically Sprague-Dawley or Wistar femur/tibia) and selecting plastic-adherent cells. Used as a syngeneic cell source for rat-defect-model implant studies and as a parallel rodent comparator to hBMSC.

## Growth conditions

- **Medium**: low-glucose DMEM or α-MEM + 10% FBS + 1% P/S; osteogenic medium adds dex + ascorbic-acid-2-phosphate + β-glycerophosphate.
- **Atmosphere**: 37 °C, 5% CO₂.
- **Passage cap**: ≤ P5–P6.

## Why pick rat-BMSC-primary for this hypothesis

- Used in [[../sources/2018-zhang-effect-of-microporosity-on-scaffolds-for]] as a parallel arm to primary-rat-calvarial-osteoblast on micro-porous HA scaffolds; the rat-femoral-defect model in that paper used the same cell source for syngeneic implantation.
