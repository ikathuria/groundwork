# MG63 — human osteosarcoma osteoblast-like line

## What it is

MG63 (ATCC CRL-1427, CVCL_0426) is a human male osteosarcoma cell line widely used as an osteoblast surrogate in materials-toxicology and scaffold-screening contexts. It is fast-growing, cheap, and tolerates a wide range of culture conditions. Like Saos-2 it is tumor-derived; unlike Saos-2 it is a weaker mineralizer and expresses an earlier-stage osteoblast phenotype.

## Growth conditions

- **Medium**: DMEM or α-MEM + 10% FBS + 1% P/S; 37 °C, 5% CO₂.
- **Subculturing**: 0.05% trypsin-EDTA at ~80% confluence.

## Why pick MG63 for this hypothesis

- A low-cost human screening line. Not a physiological osteoblast — see Saos-2 for the same critique. Pair with a primary cell or MC3T3-E1 Subclone 4 for any claim of osteoblast-proliferation effect.
