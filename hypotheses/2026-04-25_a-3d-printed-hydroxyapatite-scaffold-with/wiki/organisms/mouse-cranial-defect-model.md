# Mouse cranial defect model

## What it is

A 2–4 mm circular full-thickness defect drilled in the parietal calvaria of an adult mouse (typically C57BL/6 or BALB/c, 8–16 wk), used as a smaller, cheaper alternative to the rat calvarial defect — and as **the only practical model when the experimental hypothesis requires a transgenic, knockout, or reporter mouse background** (e.g., osteoblast-lineage GFP reporters, BMP-pathway knockouts, immunodeficient hosts for human-cell-laden scaffolds). Critical-size threshold is ~3–4 mm in adult mouse.

## Why this model for this hypothesis

- For this hypothesis (gradient-pore HA on osteoblast proliferation), the mouse cranial model is one option for in-vivo extension.
- It is **required** if the scaffold is seeded with human cells (immunodeficient mouse — Nude, NOD-SCID, NSG) before implantation, because rats lack the same immunodeficient strain options.
- Used by [[../sources/2015-ishack-bone-regeneration-in-critical-bone-defec]] which seeded scaffolds with dipyridamole-treated bone-marrow cells; defect size and time-point conventions in that paper match this model.

## Typical parameters

- **Strain**: C57BL/6 (immunocompetent), Nude / NOD-SCID / NSG (for human-cell-laden scaffolds), or transgenic backgrounds (e.g. Col1a1-GFP) for lineage tracing.
- **Age / sex**: 8–16 wk; both sexes.
- **Defect size**: 3–4 mm (critical) for HA scaffold osteoconductivity claims.
- **n**: ≥ 6–8 per group per time point.
- **Time points**: 4, 8, 12 weeks.
- **Readouts**: μCT bone-volume-fraction, histology (H&E, Masson's trichrome), occasional in-vivo bioluminescence or fluorescence imaging if the cells or matrix are tagged.
