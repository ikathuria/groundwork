# hADSC — primary human adipose-derived mesenchymal stromal cell

## What it is

Primary human adipose-derived mesenchymal stromal cells, isolated from the stromal-vascular fraction of lipoaspirate by collagenase digestion + plastic adherence. They meet the ISCT 2006 minimal MSC criteria (CD73+/CD90+/CD105+/CD34−/CD45−, tri-lineage differentiation) and are widely used as a more accessible alternative to hBMSC. Their osteogenic baseline is lower than hBMSC and the donor variability is comparably large.

## Growth conditions

- **Medium**: low-glucose DMEM or α-MEM + 10% FBS + 1% P/S; osteogenic medium adds dexamethasone + ascorbic-acid-2-phosphate + β-glycerophosphate.
- **Atmosphere**: 37 °C, 5% CO₂.
- **Passage cap**: ≤ P6 for osteogenic experiments.

## Why pick hADSC for this hypothesis

- An accessibility-of-source argument over hBMSC (lipoaspirate is far more available than marrow aspirate). Tissue-engineering scale-up arguments often invoke ADSC.
- Lower baseline osteogenic potency means smaller absolute effect sizes; not the primary screen for this hypothesis.
