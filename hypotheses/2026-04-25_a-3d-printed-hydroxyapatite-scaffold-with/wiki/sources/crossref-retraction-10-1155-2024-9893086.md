# Summary

Crossref retraction notice (Wiley/Hindawi / *BioMed Research International* vol. 2024, article 9893086, published 2024-03-20) for the original article "Strontium/Chitosan/Hydroxyapatite/Norcantharidin Composite That Inhibits Osteosarcoma and Promotes Osteogenesis In Vitro" (DOI `10.1155/2020/9825073`, published 2020). Retraction is editor-issued (author shown as "BioMed Research International"). Notice abstract is empty.

# Original claim

- A four-component composite — [[../reagents/strontium]] + [[../reagents/chitosan]] + [[../reagents/hydroxyapatite]] + [[../reagents/norcantharidin]] — simultaneously inhibits osteosarcoma cell proliferation and promotes osteogenic differentiation in vitro (a "dual-action" scaffold).

# Retraction status

- Retraction date: 2024-03-20 (publisher), 2024-03-05 (Retraction Watch entry, record-id 67040 — earlier).
- Sources: Retraction Watch record-id 67040, plus publisher-issued retraction.
- Reason: reason not stated in Crossref notice. Hindawi/Wiley issued large numbers of retractions during 2023–2024 around special-issue manipulation and paper-mill activity — this retraction is most likely part of that cleanup, but Crossref provides no narrative.
- Original authors not listed in the retraction notice.

# Failure modes flagged

- [[../failure-modes/image-reuse-unsupported-osteogenesis-claims]] — Hindawi 2020 retractions in this period frequently involve image manipulation and paper-mill traits; not confirmed by this notice but likely.
- [[../failure-modes/short-term-proliferation-vs-long-term-bone-formation]] — "in vitro" + "promotes osteogenesis" often relies on short-term ALP/ARS readouts that don't track to bone formation.
- "Dual-action" claims (cytotoxic to cancer + osteogenic to MSC/osteoblasts) are mechanistically suspect because the same Sr/norcantharidin doping that suppresses osteosarcoma should at some dose also suppress healthy osteoblasts; lack of dose–window analysis is a classic over-claim pattern.
