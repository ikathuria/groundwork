# Montoya et al. 2021 — On the road to smart biomaterials for bone research

## Summary

Conceptual and applications review proposing a four-degree taxonomy (inert → active → responsive → autonomous) of "smart" biomaterials for bone research. While the paper's primary axis is responsiveness/stimuli rather than fabrication route, it surveys advances in 3D/4D bioprinting, electronic beam melting, and robocasting for HA / CaP scaffolds, and reports pore-size-dependent biological responses that are directly relevant to the gradient-pore HA hypothesis.

## Key claims

- Smart biomaterials are redefined into four degrees of "smartness" based on bio-environment interaction, with autonomous biomaterials being the most advanced.
- A calcium-phosphate cement porous scaffold with **pore sizes around 200 μm induced higher serum ALP activity and more new bone formation in a rabbit cylindrical defect** at 4 and 12 weeks compared with scaffolds at 350–400+ μm — a counterpoint to the "bigger pore = more bone" framing.
- Other studies report that scaffolds with pore sizes around 250 μm encourage fibrous tissue ingrowth, leading to poor bone repair — a cautionary failure mode.
- 3D and 4D bioprinting and robocasting are listed as enabling technologies for smart, responsive bone scaffolds.
- HA-nanoparticle composites (e.g., γ-Fe2O3-MNPs/nHA/PLA) can deliver magnetic, mechanical, and biochemical stimuli in addition to passive osteoconduction.
- Hierarchical macropore/nanosurface topography modulates immune response (decreased inflammatory genes, increased anti-inflammatory genes) — implies a multi-scale design where pore-size gradient + surface texture together drive osteo-immunomodulation.

## Supports / contradicts

- Partially contradicts the hypothesis's premise: cites a study where ~200 μm pores outperform 350–400+ μm in vivo, suggesting the cortical-mimicking outer (~100 μm) zone of the gradient may also actively promote bone formation, not merely cell attachment.
- Reinforces the rationale for a continuous gradient rather than a single-pore-size scaffold: the literature is genuinely contested.
- Aligns with [[2025-hossen-biomimetic-strategies-for-bone-regenerat]] on hierarchical / multi-scale design.
