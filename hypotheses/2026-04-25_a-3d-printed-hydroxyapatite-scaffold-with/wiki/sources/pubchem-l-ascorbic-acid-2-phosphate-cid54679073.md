# Summary

PubChem record for L-ascorbic acid 2-phosphate (free acid form), the phosphorylated, hydrolysis-stable analog of vitamin C used in osteogenic media to drive collagen-I synthesis in osteoblasts (typical 50 µg/mL ≈ 200 µM). The cell-culture grade reagent is usually the magnesium or trisodium salt; the free-acid PubChem record (`C6H9O9P`) is the closest molecular ID. Corresponds to [[../reagents/L-ascorbic-acid-2-phosphate]].

# Hazards / Storage

- Low hazard.
- Storage: solid at –20 °C, desiccated, light-protected. Stock solutions made fresh.

# Failure modes / pitfalls

- The cell-culture supplier's product is typically the **magnesium salt** (or trisodium), not the free acid; molecular weight differs from this PubChem record. Use the supplier's MW for concentration calculations, not 256.10.
- [[../failure-modes/cytotoxic-residue-from-binders-or-photoinitiators]] — analogous concern: degraded ascorbate-2-phosphate generates dehydroascorbate, which is mildly cytotoxic at high concentrations; replace medium frequently.
- Stable to autoclaving/heat in dry form, but reconstituted solutions oxidize; prepare fresh.
