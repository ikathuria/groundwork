# de Carvalho et al. 2025 — 3D-printed CPC / mesoporous bioactive glass (MBG) scaffolds

## Summary

A direct-ink-writing study (J. Funct. Biomater., 2025) in which a commercial calcium-phosphate cement (CPC) ink (HA / α-TCP) was modified with 5 wt% mesoporous bioactive glass (MBG, pore sizes 2–50 nm, synthesised via surfactant templating) and 3D-printed into scaffolds. Mechanical / morphological / ion-release characterisation (TEM, XRD, BET, ICP-MS, SEM/EDS) plus alveolar-bone-derived human mesenchymal stem-cell biology (cell viability, DNA content, COL1A1 / OCN / RUNX2 expression) showed CPC/MBG significantly out-performed unmodified CPC at 3 and 7 days (proliferation, DNA, adhesion) and at 14 days (osteogenic gene expression). Provides a directly comparable HA/α-TCP extrusion-printing platform with quantitative day-7 / day-14 biological readouts — the closest methodological match for our experimental design.

## Key claims

- **Ink**: commercial CPC ink (HA / α-TCP) + 5 wt% MBG; control = CPC alone. Both extrusion-printed (DIW). Rheological assessment performed before printing.
- **MBG characterisation**: typical mesoporous structure with aligned microchannels, amorphous structure (TEM, XRD); surfactant-controlled pore sizes 2–50 nm.
- **Ion release (ICP-MS)**: both formulations release Ca²⁺ and PO₄³⁻; CPC/MBG additionally releases Si⁴⁺.
- **Biology — hBMSC (alveolar-bone-derived)**: cell viability, DNA content, cell adhesion/spreading **significantly greater on CPC/MBG** vs CPC at days 3 and 7 (p < 0.05). At day 14, CPC/MBG upregulated COL1A1, OCN, RUNX2 (p < 0.05).
- **Statistical framing**: Student's t-test and ANOVA with post-hoc tests, α = 5%; gene expression normalised to GAPDH (TaqMan).
- **Conclusion**: MBG modification "effectively enhances the biocompatibility and osteogenic potential of the scaffold."
