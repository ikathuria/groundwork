# Baino, Novajra & Vitale-Brovarone (2015) — Bioceramics and Scaffolds: A Winning Combination

## Summary

Frontiers in Bioengineering review of bioceramic scaffolds for tissue engineering. Covers inert/bioactive/resorbable ceramic classification (alumina, zirconia, bioactive glass, α-/β-TCP, HA), fabrication routes (foam replica, sponge replication, stereolithography, robocasting, gel-casting), and the porosity–strength tradeoff. Notable for citing **Karageorgiou & Kaplan (2005)** explicitly for the pore-size/porosity dependence of tissue integration, and for documenting bioceramic scaffolds with 400–500 μm pores produced by stereolithography that approach cancellous-bone strength after sintering.

## Key claims

- "The rate and quality of tissue integration have been related to a dependence on **scaffold pore size, porosity volume fraction, and pore interconnection** (Karageorgiou & Kaplan, 2005)" — review explicitly anchors itself to the canonical pore-size synthesis.
- A "crucial aspect" of load-bearing scaffolds is **balancing porosity with mechanical properties**.
- 45S5 Bioglass® promotes human osteoblast proliferation; at critical Si/Ca ion concentrations osteoblasts capable of differentiating proliferate within 48 h while non-progressing osteoblasts undergo apoptosis — supports bioactive bioceramic mechanisms.
- Glass–ceramic foam scaffolds (CEL2 composition, 70 vol% porosity) reach 1 MPa compressive strength; optimised processing pushes this to 5–6 MPa, in the range of cancellous bone (2–12 MPa).
- **Stereolithography-produced wollastonite glass–ceramic scaffolds with nominal pore sizes of 400 and 500 μm** survive sintering at 1200 °C with characteristic shrinkage — direct evidence the 100–500 μm window targeted by the hypothesis is achievable via SLA-based ceramic processing.
- PLGA/45S5 Bioglass® tubular composite scaffolds (~93 vol% porosity, 50–300 μm interconnected macropores, 1.5–3.0 mm wall thickness) made via TIPS show dose-dependent cell response.
- Calcium-phosphate sintering does not occur via viscous flow ⇒ may **not fully densify struts**, leaving residual microporosity — relevant micro/macro-porosity coupling.
- Robocasting / direct ink writing is identified as a member of the broad 3DP family, with thick dense struts giving high mechanical strength comparable to cortical bone.
- SLS uses CO₂ laser to sinter powdered ceramic layers — one of the standard ceramic AM routes.

## Supports / contradicts

- **Strongly supports** [[../hypothesis|the gradient-pore hypothesis]] on two fronts: (1) cites Karageorgiou & Kaplan 2005 for pore-size/porosity dependence; (2) demonstrates SLA fabrication of HA-relative bioceramic scaffolds with nominal pore sizes of 400 and 500 μm — within the upper range of the hypothesis's gradient.
- **Consistent with** [[2012-bose-recent-advances-in-bone-tissue-engineeri]] on cancellous-bone modulus targets and porosity/strength tradeoff.
