## Summary

Xie et al. review gradient biomimetic scaffolds for tendon-bone interface (TBI) regeneration. Although the explicit focus is the tendon→fibrocartilage→bone insertion gradient (rather than monolithic bone), the review's organisation of design principles is directly transferable to the present hypothesis: it parses gradients into physical structure (porosity, fibre orientation, mechanical modulus), chemical composition (mineralization degree, polymer/ECM components), biological signal (growth factors, genes), and time-gradient categories — and surveys natural polymers, synthetic polymers, and inorganic bioactive materials (calcium-phosphate ceramics, bioglass) that can carry these gradients. Includes a bibliometric analysis of materials usage. Establishes that physical-structure gradients (especially porosity) are the most-implemented and best-validated design lever for TBI, with parallels for cortical→cancellous gradients in bone.

## Key claims

- TBI repair re-tear rates clinically: 20–94% for rotator-cuff repair (Heuberer 2017; Zhao 2017) — strong clinical motivation for gradient scaffolds.
- TBI native structure: continuous gradients in cell type, ECM components, mineralization degree, and mechanical modulus from flexible tendon to rigid bone.
- Three gradient axes for design:
  - **Physical structure**: porosity (pore size, distribution), fibre orientation (aligned → random), mechanical modulus
  - **Chemical composition**: mineralization (HA loading), polymer/ECM ratio
  - **Biological signal**: growth factor (BMP-2 for bone end, TGF-β for tendon end), gene therapy
  - **Time-gradient**: temporally staged release schedules
- Materials surveyed: natural polymers (collagen, silk fibroin, chitosan), synthetic (PCL, PLGA, PU), inorganic bioactive (calcium phosphate ceramics including HA, β-TCP, bioglass).
- Fabrication: electrospinning is dominant for fibre/composition gradients; AM (especially DLP, FDM, robocasting, multi-material extrusion) is dominant for porosity-gradient scaffolds, especially with ceramic loadings.
- Bibliometric analysis (visualised in figures) shows PCL + HA composites are the most-published combination for TBI gradient scaffolds.
- Reviews specific successful gradient designs combining HA mineralization gradient with porosity gradient — directly relevant for HA-radial-gradient hypothesis.
