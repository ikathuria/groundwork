# Hossen et al. 2025 — Biomimetic Strategies for Bone Regeneration: Smart Scaffolds and Multiscale Cues

## Summary

Recent (late-2025 / collection-2026) review of biomimetic scaffold strategies that integrate hierarchical pore architecture, biochemical/mechanical signals, and adaptive responsiveness. Calls out additive manufacturing (SLA, SLS, FDM, inkjet) as the enabling fabrication for functionally graded constructs and explicitly endorses pore-size gradients spanning ~100–300 μm to support cellular infiltration and angiogenesis. Strongly biased toward smart / hierarchical / multi-scale designs, providing recent supporting context for the hypothesis.

## Key claims

- "Pore sizes of 100–300 μm support cellular infiltration, while ~300 μm pores strongly promote angiogenesis."
- A scaffold must integrate angiogenic cues *and* structural porosity, especially pores of ~300 μm, to support robust vessel ingrowth and accelerate defect repair.
- Light-based 3D printing (DLP/SLA) enables accurate control over pore size, microchannels, and stiffness, supporting biomimetic designs that guide cell organisation and tissue maturation.
- AM techniques (SLM, SLS, SLA) enable customised geometries, **pore gradients**, and mechanical tuning.
- 3D-printed CaP and HA scaffolds are promising graft alternatives but their intrinsic osteoinductivity remains limited — argues for biochemical functionalisation (BMP-2, growth factors).
- Bone has a hierarchical structure where collagen fibrils interwoven with HA crystals form a composite balancing strength and flexibility — direct biomimetic justification for the cortical-/cancellous-mimicking gradient.
- Multiscale design (nano- to macro-) supports both osteogenic activity and immune regulation; scaffolds must avoid cytotoxicity and minimise inflammation.
