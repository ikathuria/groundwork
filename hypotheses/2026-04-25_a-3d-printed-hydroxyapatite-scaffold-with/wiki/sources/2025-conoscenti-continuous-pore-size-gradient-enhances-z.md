## Summary

Conoscenti et al. fabricated a continuous-gradient-porosity poly-L-lactic-acid (PLLA) scaffold using thermally induced phase separation (TIPS) with asymmetric Peltier-controlled cooling, producing a continuous gradient from ~70 μm (slow-cooled, "chondral") to ~200 μm (fast-cooled, "osseous") pore size at 93 ± 0.5% porosity. Bone-marrow human MSCs were seeded and cultured in a biphasic bioreactor that delivered chondrogenic medium to the small-pore side and osteogenic medium to the large-pore side. The gradient PLLA outperformed a non-porous GelMA control on zonal differentiation: small pores upregulated SOX9, ACAN, COL2 and supported GAG deposition (Alcian blue), while large pores upregulated RUNX2, BSP2, OPN and Ca deposition (Alizarin red). Critically, even when both ends were exposed to a single medium, pore geometry alone biased differentiation, providing strong direct evidence that continuous radial pore-size gradients (not just biochemical gradients) drive a corresponding gradient in stem-cell fate.

## Key claims

- Continuous TIPS gradient: **70 μm (slow cooling, 1 °C/min to 30 °C, 20 min hold) → 200 μm (fast quench to 30 °C, 50 min hold)** along scaffold thickness, verified by SEM and microCT; porosity uniform at 93 ± 0.5% (gas pycnometer).
- Compressive moduli were in MPa range; uniform-pore scaffolds were *stiffer* than gradient (small-pore stiffest), but all comparable to in vivo cartilage range.
- Cell seeding efficiency 22.8 ± 1.3% (cylindrical scaffold 3.5 mm × 8.5 mm, 80,000 BM-hMSCs per scaffold). MTS counts more than doubled by day 7 — no PLLA cytotoxicity.
- **Pore size alone biases differentiation under identical media:** small-pore region upregulated chondrogenic genes under chondrogenic medium and resisted calcification; large-pore region upregulated osteogenic genes under osteogenic medium and showed higher Ca deposition.
- Mechanism proposed: small pores increase cell–cell contact and density that drives chondrogenesis; larger pores allow vascular invasion and oxygenation that supports osteogenesis.
- 3 BM-hMSC donor pools (n = 3 patients per pool, mixed sex/age).
- Statistical significance limited by small donor count, but trends were directionally consistent and significant for SOX9 and BSP2 between zones.

## Supports / contradicts

- **Supports** [[2024-diez-escudero-in-vitro-evaluation-of-pore-size-graded-bone]] (Daskalakis): both demonstrate a gradient in cellular response along a gradient-pore scaffold.
- **Supports** [[2022-yang-gaussian-curvature-driven-direction-of-c]]: both attribute differentiation cues to scaffold geometry/topology rather than biochemistry alone.
- **Tension with the hypothesis under test**: the gradient direction here is *opposite* in sign to the proposed cortical-mimicking 100→500 μm range — Conoscenti's small-pore region drives chondrogenesis, not cortical-bone-mimicking osteoblast attachment.
- **Cited by** the [[2025-liu-gradient-scaffolds-in-bone-soft-tissue-i]] and [[2025-xie-recent-advances-in-gradient-biomimetic-s]] review framings.
