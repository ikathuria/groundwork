# Summary

Crossref retraction notice (Elsevier / *Heliyon* 10(17): e36493, retracted 2024-09-15; original published 2024-08-16) for "Eggshell derived scaffold of hydroxyapatite-ammonium bicarbonate nano-composite: Bioactivity and cytotoxicity studies" by Oladipupo et al. Funded by Taif University (TU-DSPP-2024-13). Article retracted within ~one month of publication. Crossref notice **does not include a narrative reason**. A subsequent 2026 erratum (`10.1016/j.heliyon.2026.e44839`) also recorded.

# Original claim

- A scaffold derived from eggshell-sourced [[../reagents/hydroxyapatite]] combined with [[../reagents/ammonium-bicarbonate]] (presumably as a porogen) is bioactive (e.g., simulated-body-fluid apatite formation) and non-cytotoxic.

# Retraction status

- Retraction date: 2024-09-15 (Elsevier publisher-issued).
- Source: publisher only (no Retraction Watch record-id in this notice).
- Reason: reason not stated in Crossref notice. Rapid (~30-day) retraction commonly indicates plagiarism, duplicate submission, or authorship dispute, but is not confirmed here.
- Multi-author roster across Saudi institutions; reference list shows many self-citations from the [[../reagents/eggshell-calcium-carbonate|eggshell-CaCO₃]]-derived-HA literature.

# Failure modes flagged

- [[../failure-modes/batch-to-batch-HA-stoichiometry-variation]] — eggshell-derived HA varies in calcium-deficiency, carbonate substitution, and trace-metal content based on shell source and calcination protocol; "biogenic HA" papers are repeat retraction targets for under-characterized starting material.
- [[../failure-modes/cytotoxic-residue-from-binders-or-photoinitiators]] — ammonium bicarbonate as a porogen leaves residue if not fully thermally decomposed; cytotoxicity claims must be paired with residue-quantification.
- [[../failure-modes/image-reuse-unsupported-osteogenesis-claims]] — default suspicion; not confirmed.
