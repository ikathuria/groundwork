# Daghrery et al. 2026 — Advances in 3D Printed Scaffolds for Periodontal Regeneration

## Summary

Periodontal-focused review of 3D-printed scaffold-guided regeneration (SGPR) covering composite scaffolds, ECM-mimetic hydrogels, ion-releasing ceramics, and multiphasic / anisotropic / functionally-graded designs that mimic the cementum–PDL–bone interface. **Scope note:** the primary application is periodontal not skeletal bone; however, the bone-compartment design choices, multiphasic gradient strategies, and PCL/HA composite findings transfer directly to the gradient-pore HA hypothesis.

## Key claims (bone-applicable)

- Multiphasic 3D-printed scaffolds successfully reproduce the cementum–PDL–bone interface; e.g., personalised PCL/HA scaffolds with **Phase A (cementum/dentin, 100 μm), Phase B (PDL, 600 μm), Phase C (bone, 300 μm)** — a documented proof-of-concept for layered-gradient pore-size architecture in CaP composites.
- Melt electrowriting (MEW), extrusion, and inkjet printing all enable patient-specific multiphasic and anisotropic scaffolds; aligned MEW PCL fibers with **500 μm strand spacing** increased RUNX2 and IL-10 gene expression while decreasing pro-inflammatory markers in vitro.
- MEW PCL scaffolds support long-term 3D coculture of osteoblasts and PDL fibroblasts, producing a continuous mineralised gradient and PDL-like insertions — supports the proposition that gradient architectures actively drive cell-organisational outcomes.
- Higher porosity enhances human osteoblast-like cell proliferation and protein synthesis (IL-6, IL-8); cited as a directional confirmation that increasing pore-window in the cancellous-mimicking core supports proliferation.
- Inkjet printing has lower resolution (5–100 μm) and slower print speed; longer crosslinking times can damage cells.
- HA, β-TCP, magnesium phosphate, bioactive glasses are all systematically evaluated for periodontal hard-tissue compartments.
- Future directions: in situ and 4D bioprinting, immuno-instructive and prevascularised constructs, CAD-from-clinical-imaging — all extend to skeletal applications.

## Supports / contradicts

- Supports the hypothesis: multi-phasic / multi-zone CaP-PCL architectures with explicit pore-size partitioning (100 / 300 / 600 μm) are documented and effective. The gradient-pore HA hypothesis is essentially a continuous-gradient generalisation of this discrete-zone design.
- The 100 μm / 600 μm / 300 μm phase assignment is *different* from the hypothesis (smaller-to-larger from cortical-mimicking outer to cancellous-mimicking core) but reinforces that small (100 μm) and large (≥300 μm) pores can coexist in one printed construct.
- **Scope caveat:** primary application is periodontal regeneration, where the interfacial complexity (mineralised cementum + soft PDL + alveolar bone) is the dominant design driver; transfer to a single-tissue (skeletal bone) gradient is straightforward but not 1:1.
- Aligns with [[2023-koushik-bone-tissue-engineering-scaffolds-functi]], [[2025-tang-advances-in-3d-printed-scaffolds-for-bon]] on multi-zone / hierarchical design.

- "Personalized PCL/hydroxyapatite scaffolds comprised of multiphases; Phase A (cementum/dentin, 100 μm), Phase B (PDL, 600 μm), and Phase C (bone, 300 μm), the scaffold promoted the formation of PDL-like fibers integrated with bone- and cementum/dentin-like tissues in subcutaneous [implantation]…"
- "Aligned MEW PCL fibers with large strand spacing (500 μm) increased the gene expression of RUNX2 and IL10 while decreasing pro-inflammatory markers in vitro."
- "Hierarchically graded tissues feature a spatially controlled distribution of different cell types, ECM components, and functional elements, arranged in an ordered manner across macro- to microscale gradients that mirror the complexity of physiological tissues."
- "Higher porosity enhanced human osteoblast-like cell proliferation and protein synthesis, with increased protein concentration of cytokines IL-6 and IL-8."
- "MEW PCL scaffolds supported long-term 3D coculture of osteoblasts and PDL fibroblasts, resulting in a continuous mineralized gradient and PDL-like insertions."
