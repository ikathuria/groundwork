## Summary

Liu et al. review gradient scaffolds for bone–soft-tissue interface engineering (osteochondral, tendon-bone, ligament-bone), classifying scaffolds by gradient cue type — biochemical, stiffness, porous structure, fibre structure — and surveying fabrication routes — microfluidic patterning, mask-based photolithography, molecular migration, electrospinning, 3D printing, temperature control (TIPS). Although the review's emphasis is on interface (bi-tissue) regeneration rather than monolithic bone scaffolds, its taxonomy of gradient-construction strategies, its discussion of stiffness gradients matched to in-vivo modulus profiles, and its compilation of growth-factor-loaded gradient designs are directly relevant to the proposed HA radial-gradient scaffold. Provides supporting context that gradient scaffolds outperform abrupt-transition biphasic/triphasic designs for cellular integration and stress dissipation.

## Key claims

- Bone–soft-tissue interfaces (cartilage, tendon, ligament) account for >170,000 rotator-cuff tears and >300,000 shoulder/foot/ankle surgeries annually in the U.S.; re-tear rates 15–94% indicate poor functional regeneration with current biphasic grafts.
- Evolution of designs: single-phase → biphasic → triphasic → multiphasic → continuous gradient.
- Continuous gradient scaffolds out-perform abrupt-transition multiphasic scaffolds because progressive stiffness/composition transitions reduce stress concentrations at interface boundaries.
- Five fabrication categories surveyed:
  - **Microfluidic patterning** — hydrogel composition gradients
  - **Mask-based photolithography** — UV-dose-driven crosslinking gradient
  - **Molecular migration** — diffusive equilibration in solution
  - **Electrospinning** — fibre alignment / composition gradients
  - **3D printing** — multi-material extrusion, voxel-level control
  - **Temperature control / TIPS** — thermal-history-driven pore-size gradient (cited Conoscenti et al. and others)
- Gradient cue types: biochemical (growth-factor immobilization, mineralization gradient), stiffness (modulus gradient), porous structure (pore-size gradient), fibrous structure (fibre orientation/diameter gradient).
- Cell culture in gradient scaffolds: spatially zoned differentiation observed when MSCs span a gradient region; growth-factor gradients (TGF-β, BMP-2) layered onto structural gradients improve specificity.
- Compares natural polymers (collagen, silk, chitosan), synthetics (PCL, PLGA, PU), and bioactive ceramics (HA, β-TCP, bioglass).

## Notable quotes

- "Compared to other reconstruction methods, which often struggle to address the stiffness differences between various tissues, gradient scaffolds gradually transition the mechanical properties of materials, effectively alleviating stress concentrations." (Introduction)
- "Gradient biomimetic scaffolds used for interface repair demonstrate remarkable potential to mimic the intricate micro- and nanoscale tissue transitions in natural tissues. This is primarily attributed to the biomimetic, progressive, and continuous transitions in composition and mechanical properties exhibited by gradient scaffold designs." (Introduction)
- "Graft design has significantly improved, evolving from simple single-phase scaffolds to biphasic, triphasic, multiphasic, and, most recently, gradient scaffolds." (Introduction)
