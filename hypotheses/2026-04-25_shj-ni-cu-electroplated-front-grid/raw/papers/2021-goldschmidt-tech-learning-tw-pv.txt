                                                                                                                    Broader context
                                                                                                                    Photovoltaics – the direct conversion of solar radiation into electricity – is the major energy conversion technology for cost efficient climate change mitigation.
                                                                                                                    To achieve this goal a huge number of systems must be installed, which is associated with a high resource demand, e.g in terms of energy, float-glass, metals,
                                                                                                                    and capital investments, as well as with associated greenhouse gas emissions. In this paper, we investigate whether the projected growth is feasible from a
                                                                                                                    resource point of view – and if so, under which conditions. In this analysis, we consider that the photovoltaic technology is constantly evolving, and new
                                                                                                                    generations of systems are more efficient and consume less resources during production. We find that without technological learning, resource limitations will
                                                                                                                    most likely occur. Those limitations can be avoided if technological learning continues with its current speed also in the long-term future. Already now,
                                                                                                                    technological solutions like perovskite-based tandem solar cells are foreseeable that promise high efficiencies at low-costs and low resource consumption.
                                                                                                                    Therefore, we conclude that current and future investments must not only be targeted at capacity expansion but also at upholding the currently high rate of
                                                                                                                    innovation.
