  ABSTRACT                                                                                                                                     PV
  In any solar cell process, the metallization step is critical as it often sets conditions and limitations for the other process              Modules
  steps. The main metallization technique used today in Si solar cell production is screen-printing of metallic pastes;
  namely, Ag pastes for the front side, Al pastes for most of the rear side, and Ag or Ag-Al pastes for the solder pads at the                 Power
  rear. While these techniques are clearly robust and convenient, they have limitations. Therefore, alternatives are being                     Generation
  investigated. A technique that is presently finding its way into production is two-step metallization with Ag plating.
  Another more radical approach is to avoid printing altogether, instead using some kind of ablation followed by plating.                      Market
  For the rear, the full Al-BSF is being replaced by dielectric passivation and local Al-alloyed contacts. Back-contacted                      Watch
  cells are increasingly being introduced in production, and they pose very specific challenges to metallization. For
  the sustainability of Si photovoltaics, it is crucial that the future metallization solutions only make use of abundantly
  available and non-toxic materials.


Introduction                                      a
Thick-film metallization by means
of screen-printing is the contacting
technique employed by more than 85%
of photovoltaic solar cell manufacturers.
The majority of solar cells produced
worldwide are fabricated using silver
thick-film contacts on the front and
aluminum thick-film contacts on the rear
side. In addition, solder pads on the rear
are formed using Ag and Ag-Al pastes.
A process simplification in the front and
rear contact formation was enabled by the         b
development of the silicon nitride firing-
through process of the front side Ag paste
and the formation of the back surface field
(BSF) by an Al paste during the same firing
step. This has elevated screen-printing to
be classed as one of the key process steps
in silicon solar cell manufacturing.
