# Retraction: Graded bandgap perovskite solar cells

**Ergen, O., Gilbert, S. M., Pham, T., Turner, S. J., Tan, M. T. Z., Worsley, M. A., Zettl, A.** — *Nature Materials, 17, 204, 2018* (retraction note for *Nature Materials 16, 522–525, 2017*).

## Summary

Author-initiated retraction of a high-profile Nature Materials letter that had reported a "graded bandgap" perovskite solar cell — two perovskite layers separated by an h-BN monolayer on a graphene aerogel — with claimed champion efficiencies above 21%. The authors retracted the Letter ~13 months after print due to (1) large device-to-device variability in I–V and spectral-response data on nominally identical devices, and (2) rapid time evolution of key photovoltaic parameters. All co-authors agreed. **Cautionary precedent for any PV efficiency / reliability claim — including this hypothesis** — that asserts champion performance + 1000 h damp-heat stability on a small set of devices: variability across nominally identical samples and time-evolution of the I–V curve are the exact symptoms that *must* be monitored and disclosed.

## Key claims

- Original 2017 paper claimed graded-bandgap perovskite architecture on graphene aerogel.
- Authors retracted on the basis of "large variability of the current–voltage and spectral response of nominally identical devices and the rapid time evolution of key photovoltaic parameters."
- All authors agreed with the retraction.
- The conclusions about the *performance* of these architectures could not be drawn from the available data.

## Failure modes flagged

- [[failure-modes/non-reproducibility-in-solar-cell-claims]] — this is the canonical example. The mechanism (cell-to-cell variability + time-evolution of I–V) is structurally identical to the risk facing any plated-Cu SHJ champion-efficiency or 1000 h DH claim.
- [[failure-modes/Voc-degradation-Cu-contamination]] — analogous: PV claims that look stable in a hero curve but drift over hours-to-days are the exact pattern Cu contamination would produce; this retraction is the cautionary precedent for *not* publishing on a single device.

## Supports / contradicts

- **Supports:** the hypothesis's experimental design discipline — the retraction is implicitly cited as the *reason* Pass-3 mandates N=5–10 cells per arm + checkpoint IV at 0/250/500/750/1000 h, *not* a single-device measurement.

## Notable quotes

> "The authors of the study are retracting this Letter due to concerns about the reproducibility of the photovoltaic architecture performance presented, and with the interpretation of the data included in the manuscript and Supplementary Information." — *Retraction note*

> "The large variability of the current–voltage and spectral response of nominally identical devices and the rapid time evolution of key photovoltaic parameters undermine the authors' confidence in the conclusions that can be drawn at this stage regarding the performance of these architectures." — *Retraction note*
