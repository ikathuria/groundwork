# Sulfuric acid — Fisher / Acros Organics SDS

## Summary
Fisher Scientific (Acros Organics) GHS/OSHA SDS for ACS-grade sulfuric acid, CAS 7664-93-9, ≥95% purity. Sulfuric acid is the conductivity-boosting acid in the Cu plating bath (Cupracid family — 50–70 g/L per [[atotech-cupracid-ultra-a-tds]]) and is also widely used to acidify Ni sulfamate plating baths and in pre-plating cleans. This SDS provides the corrosive-handling, hygiene, and ventilation guidance for the experiment-plan safety section. Created 2009-06-23, last revised 2025-12-19 (Revision 10).

## Key product specs
- Identity: Sulfuric acid, CAS 7664-93-9.
- Composition: ≥95% H2SO4.
- Formula: H2SO4 (H2O4S); molecular weight 98.07 g/mol.
- Appearance: colorless, odorless liquid.
- Hazards (US OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200 (2024)):
  - Skin Corrosion/Irritation Category 1.
  - Serious Eye Damage Category 1.
  - Specific Target Organ Toxicity (single exposure) Category 3 — respiratory system.
  - Signal word: Danger.
  - California Prop 65 cancer warning (acid mists).
- Physical properties:
  - Melting point 10 °C.
  - Boiling point 290 °C; decomposition temperature 340 °C.
  - pH ≈ 1 (1 N aq).
  - Density / SG 1.840.
  - Viscosity 21 mPa·s at 25 °C.
  - Vapor pressure 1 mmHg at 146 °C.
  - Water-miscible; reacts violently with water (always add acid to water).
- Exposure: ACGIH TLV TWA 0.2 mg/m³, OSHA PEL TWA 1 mg/m³ (vacated), NIOSH IDLH 15 mg/m³ REL 1 mg/m³ TWA.
- Storage: tightly closed in dry, cool, well-ventilated, locked-up place; corrosives area; incompatible with strong oxidizers, bases, organic materials, reducing agents, finely powdered metals, peroxides; may evolve flammable hydrogen on contact with metals.
- Fire-fighting: nonflammable; do NOT use water; hazardous combustion products = hydrogen, sulfur oxides.
- NFPA: Health 3 / Flammability 0 / Instability 2 / Physical hazards W (water-reactive).
- Catalog numbers (multi-format SKU): AC424520000 / AC424520025 / AC424520026 / AC424520100 / AC424525000 / AC424525001 / AC424528000.
- Pricing: not in SDS.
