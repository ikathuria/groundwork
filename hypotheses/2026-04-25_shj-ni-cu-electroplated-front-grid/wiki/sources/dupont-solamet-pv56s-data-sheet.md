# DuPont Solamet PV56S — back-side solderable Ag paste (low-Ag, non-fire-through)

## Summary
DuPont Solamet PV56S is a back-side solderable silver paste optimized for localized back-surface-field (LBSF) cells — high adhesion to SiNx with reduced silver consumption, designed to suppress carrier recombination at the passivation layer (high Voc). Co-fireable with front side (n-type) Ag (PV17x/PV18x) and with back-side Al (PV36x). Like [[dupont-solamet-pv21a-tech-sheet]], PV56S is a fire-through-compatible composition (peak firing) so not directly applicable to SHJ; it is included as a reference for industry-standard solderable Ag tabbing pads, providing the "ribbon-soldered to silver pad" benchmark which the hypothesis's Ni/Cu/Sn(Ag) cap stack must match.

## Key product specs
- Application: standard screen-print process, 180–250 mm/sec print speed.
- Screen: 200 or 250 mesh stainless steel.
- Drying: vertical dryer 170–230 °C ×10 min, or IR belt dryer 220–270 °C ×30 sec.
- Firing: rapid spike firing; thermal budget above 600 °C kept ≤8 sec for optimum contact.
- Soldering: industry-standard non-clean L0/M0 flux (ANSI/J-STD-004); compatible with 60Sn/40Pb, 62Sn/36Pb/2Ag, 96.5Sn/3.5Ag ribbons.
- Physical:
  - Viscosity 50–100 Pa·s (Brookfield HADV, SC4-14/6R, 10 rpm, 25 °C).
  - Solids 59–61% at 750 °C.
  - Fineness of grind <12 µm / <6 µm.
  - Thinner: DuPont 9450.
  - Shelf life 6 months.
- Cadmium-free.
- Storage: 5–25 °C; not >30 °C and not <0 °C.
- Pricing: not listed.
