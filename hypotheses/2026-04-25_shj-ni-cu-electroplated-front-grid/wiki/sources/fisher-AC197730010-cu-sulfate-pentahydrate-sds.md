# Copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate — Fisher / Acros Organics SDS

## Summary
Fisher Scientific (Acros Organics) GHS/OSHA SDS for copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate (blue vitriol), CAS 7758-99-8. This is the canonical Cu source used to make up the acid Cu plating bath (Cupracid family, see [[atotech-cupracid-ultra-a-tds]]; Atotech TDS specifies 195–255 g/L CuSO4·5H2O). The SDS was created 2009-10-19, last revised 2025-12-19 (Revision 10) — current as of plan date. Reagent grade, ≥95% purity. Provides identity, hazard classification, storage, and handling required for the lab safety section of the experiment plan.

## Key product specs
- Identity: Copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate, CAS 7758-99-8.
- Synonym: Blue Vitriol.
- Composition: ≥95% Copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate (1:1:5); CAS 7758-98-7 cupric sulfate (anhydrous) listed as related component.
- Formula: CuSO4·5H2O (CuO4S.5H2O); molecular weight 249.68 g/mol.
- Appearance: blue crystalline solid, odorless.
- Catalog numbers: AC197730000 / AC197730010 (50 g) / AC197730050 / AC197730250.
- Recommended use: laboratory chemicals (advised against food/drug/pesticide/biocidal).
- Hazards (US OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200 (2024)):
  - Acute oral toxicity Category 4 (Harmful if swallowed).
  - Serious Eye Damage Category 1.
  - Signal word: Danger.
  - HNOC: very toxic to aquatic life with long-lasting effects.
- Physical properties:
  - Melting point 110 °C.
  - pH 3.5–4.5 (5% aq solution).
  - Water solubility 320 g/L (20 °C).
  - Vapor pressure 7.3 mmHg at 25 °C.
- Exposure: ACGIH TLV TWA 1 mg/m³ (Cu), NIOSH IDLH 100 mg/m³, REL 1 mg/m³ TWA.
- Storage: tightly closed in dry, cool, well-ventilated place; under inert atmosphere; air sensitive. Incompatible with strong oxidizing agents.
- Toxicity: LD50 oral (rat) 960 mg/kg; LD50 dermal (rabbit) >8 g/kg.
- NFPA: Health 2 / Flammability 0 / Instability 1.
- Pricing: not in SDS.
