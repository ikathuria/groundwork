# Photovoltaics Report — Fraunhofer ISE (2024 edition, updated 31 Oct 2025)

## Summary
Fraunhofer ISE's flagship periodic Photovoltaics Report. Pulls together up-to-date data on global PV installation (456 GW in 2023, ~602 GW in 2024; cumulative 2156.5 GW end-2024), production by technology (98% c-Si in 2024 with 70% n-type wafers; mc-Si effectively zero), record cell efficiencies (27.8% mono-Si, 26.9% perovskite, 47.6% III-V multijunction), and the long-running 25.7%/doubling module-price learning rate over 44 years. Most relevant for this hypothesis: explicitly states that "n-type TopCon and Heterojunction replaces p-type PERC" in mass-production module efficiency tables, and the LCA section names "replacing silver (Ag) by copper (Cu) or aluminium (Al)" as the canonical solution to the silver bottleneck — directly underwriting the SHJ-Cu plating thesis at the industry-data level. Also documents EU PV manufacturing landscape (cell capacity <5 GWp/a, no active ingot/wafer, one polysilicon manufacturer) which frames the strategic case for Cu-plating tool localization.

## Key claims
- Worldwide PV cumulative installation: 1581 GW (end-2023) → 2156.5 GW (end-2024); EU at 251 GW; Germany at 92 GW.
- Worldwide PV module production 2024: ~700 GWp (ITRPV: 703 GW), of which 687 GW mono-Si, 16 GW thin film, 0 multi-Si.
- China share of module production 2024: ~86%; Asia overall ~96%; controls >95% of certain components (ingots, wafers).
- 2024 ITRPV breakdown: 98% c-Si, 70% n-type wafers (TOPCon, SHJ, IBC).
- Record lab cell efficiencies (Solar Cell Efficiency Tables v66, April 2025): 27.8% mono-Si, 26.9% perovskite, 26.0% mono-Si module, 23.4% CIGS, 21.0% CdTe, 47.6% III-V multijunction (concentrator).
- Mono-Si commercial module efficiency 16% → >24% over the last 10 years; weighted-average c-Si module efficiency Q4-2024 = 22.7%.
- Module price learning rate 25.7% per doubling of cumulative production over 44 years; 2024 ASP ≈ 0.13 US$/Wp.
- Silicon usage cut from ~16 g/Wp (2004) to ~2.0 g/Wp (2024) via thinner wafers (~140 µm), diamond wire saws, larger ingots.
- Energy Payback Time: 0.9–1.1 yr in Europe (system manufactured in Europe); EPBT learning rate 15.7%/doubling.
- LCA: PV module mass breakdown shows Ag/PbO + Ag/Al/PbO ≈ 0.05% of module mass (~5 g for an 11.6 kg/m² module).
- Silver-reduction roadmap (LCA section): "Multi-busbars with more precise application of the silver paste; busbar-less cells; Replacing silver (Ag) by copper (Cu) or aluminium (Al)" — direct industry-level endorsement of the Cu-plating route.
- Lead reduction roadmap also calls for "lead-free solders or alternative contacting; alternative interconnection using electrically conductive adhesive (ECA)" — directly relevant to SHJ low-T module assembly.
- PV LCOE 2024: global weighted average 39 €/MWh (5th–95th percentile: 29–112 €/MWh).
- EU PV cell capacity <5 GWp/a; no active ingot/wafer manufacturing; one polysilicon producer; module capacity dispersed across many <1 GWp/a manufacturers.
