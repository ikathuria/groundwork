# Photovoltaics Report

**Fraunhofer ISE, PSE Projects** — *Fraunhofer ISE annual study, latest edition (2024 results, updated 03/2025–09/2025)*. DOI: —

## Summary

Industry-canonical PV market context document, updated multiple times per year by Fraunhofer ISE: cumulative installed capacity, c-Si market share, learning-curve / experience curve, cost structure, regional production, and cell-technology efficiency records. Cited by every PV review. Provides the macro baseline that the SHJ Ni/Cu hypothesis lives in: 2.16 TW cumulative deployed by end of 2024, 98% c-Si production, 70% n-type wafer share, ~$0.13/Wp module ASP, 25.7% module-price experience-curve learning rate.

## Key claims

- **Cumulative PV capacity:** 2,156.5 GWp installed worldwide at end of 2024 (per IEA-PVPS); 1,581 GW at end of 2023.
- **Annual installations:** 602 GW newly installed in 2024 (IEA), up from 456 GW in 2023.
- **CAGR 2014–2024:** 27% per year cumulative installations.
- **c-Si dominance:** silicon-wafer-based technology = ~98% of total production in 2024; **70% n-type wafer share** (per ITRPV) — i.e. SHJ + TOPCon already dominate new fab additions.
- **Cell technology efficiency records (lab, ≥1 cm²):** 27.8% monocrystalline c-Si (single-junction lab record); 26.9% perovskite; 23.4% CIGS; 21.0% CdTe. Record c-Si module 26.0%.
- **Commercial mono c-Si module efficiency:** ~16% in 2014 → >24% in 2024.
- **Cost / learning curve:** 25.7% module-price reduction per doubling of cumulative global production over 44 years; 2024 average selling price ~$0.13 / Wp.
- **Cost split (Fraunhofer ISE 2024 c-Si module manufacturing):** 0.117 US$/Wp PERC, 0.120 US$/W n-type modules — n-type only ~3% more expensive at module level despite higher silver content.
- **Silicon consumption:** 16 g/Wp (2004) → ~2.0 g/Wp (2024) — wafer-thinning has worked; metallization cost is now the next frontier (the SHJ Ni/Cu hypothesis's domain).
- **Concentration of supply:** ~96% of modules from Asia, ~86% China alone; Chinese ingot/wafer market share >95% — relevant to supply-chain risk that motivates Western Cu-plating commercializations (SunDrive, Maxwell, CSEM) the hypothesis competes with.
- **Energy payback time:** ~1 year for systems in southern Europe.
- **Module manufacturing capacity:** ~700 GWp module production in 2024 globally.
