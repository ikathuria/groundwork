1. Hypothesis

Original question · 2026-04-25
Supplementing C57BL/6 mice with Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG for 4 weeks will reduce intestinal permeability by at least 30 % compared to untreated controls, measured by plasma FITC-dextran levels after oral gavage.

Refined

InterventionDaily oral gavage of viable LGG (ATCC 53103) at 1×10⁹ CFU/mouse/day in 200 µL sterile PBS, for 28 consecutive days.
OutcomePlasma FITC-dextran 4 kDa at 4 h after a single 600 mg/kg gavage; fluorescence (485/520 nm) against a same-day standard curve in pooled naïve mouse plasma.
ThresholdLGG arm mean ≤ 70 % of PBS mean (≥ 30 % reduction); two-sided Mann–Whitney U at α = 0.05.
MechanismTight-junction up-regulation (ZO-1, occludin, claudin-1/3/4) + claudin-2 ↓; soluble factor p40 (Msp1) activates EGFR-PI3K/Akt; competitive exclusion; SCFA-mediated colonocyte support.
ControlPBS-vehicle C57BL/6J, equal volume, daily; secondary heat-killed LGG arm (optional, dissects viability dependence).

2. Literature QC

The honest novelty assessment the brief asks for.

similar-work-exists · with major caveats

Extensive published LGG → intestinal-barrier literature exists, but ~85 % of evidence is from challenge models (DSS, alcohol, HFD, NEC, post-weaning stress), not unstressed adult C57BL/6. The closest published precedent (Singh 2014, LGG fermented milk in aging mice) was retracted in 2024. The unstressed-baseline question is therefore under-explored AND the closest match is unreliable. Generalising published effect sizes to this design is unwarranted.

LGG stimulates dietary tryptophan-dependent production of barrier-protecting methylnicotinamide
Suntornsaratoon et al. · 2024 · doi:10.1016/j.jcmgh.2024.04.003
Closest in design — uses LGG + FITC-dextran + C57BL/6 with healthy + DSS arms; mechanistic depth via tryptophan → MNA → barrier.
Neonatal colonization of mice with LGG promotes intestinal development
Yan, Liu, Cao, Polk, et al. · 2017 · doi:10.1080/19490976.2016.1257126
Yan/Polk lineage; in vivo C57BL/6, FD-4 measurement. Neonatal-imprint design rather than adult unchallenged, but mechanism is well characterised.
Improvement in Th1/Th2 immune homeostasis ... LGG fermented milk in aging mice
Singh et al. · 2014 · doi:10.1007/s11357-014-9686-4 · Retracted 2024
Most direct published precedent — and it didn't hold up. Listed for transparency: this is the kind of result this hypothesis is testing, and the retraction is itself signal.

8. Validation

Pre-registered on OSF before unblinding. Result published regardless of direction.

Primary success criterion

Mean plasma FD-4 in LGG arm ≤ 70 % of PBS arm mean (≥ 30 % reduction); two-sided Mann–Whitney U, p < 0.05.

Secondary corroborations · FDR-corrected

  • ZO-1, occludin, claudin-1 protein up in LGG-arm ileum (WB)
  • Claudin-2 down (pore-forming)
  • Plasma LBP lower in LGG arm (consistent direction)
  • Caecal butyrate higher in LGG arm
  • 16S confirms LGG colonisation in treatment arm AND absence from control arm

Quality gates · any failure invalidates the assay

  • Dose-day CFU within 80–120 % of design every day
  • FD-4 standard curve R² > 0.99 with documented matrix correction
  • No haemolysed plasma samples in primary analysis
  • 16S faecal LGG present in 100 % of LGG samples and 0 % of control samples
  • All animals survive without humane-endpoint events

Reporting commitment

Pre-registered on OSF before unblinding. Results published regardless of direction (including null) within 6 months of analysis lock.
