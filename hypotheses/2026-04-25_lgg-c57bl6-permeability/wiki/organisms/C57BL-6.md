# Mus musculus, C57BL/6 inbred strain

The hypothesis pre-registers **C57BL/6J** specifically (JAX 000664) — not the generic "C57BL/6" — for two reasons:

1. The **C57BL/6J vs C57BL/6N substrain confounder** is real: the *Nnt* deletion in 6J alters mitochondrial NADP(H) cycling and downstream redox tone; documented baseline microbiome and metabolic differences from 6N. Mixing substrains across arms (or vs published literature using the other) silently invalidates comparisons. See [[../failure-modes/C57BL-6J-vs-N-substrain-confounder]].
2. The **vendor-of-origin microbiome baseline** matters: JAX, Taconic, and Charles River mice arrive with measurably different commensal communities. Two LGG studies on "C57BL/6 mice" from different vendors are not the same experiment. See [[../failure-modes/vendor-of-origin-microbiome-baseline]].

## Operational notes (this hypothesis)

- **Source**: Jackson 000664 (C57BL/6J), single shipment, single age cohort.
- **Sex / age**: both sexes; 6–10 weeks at study start.
- **Acclimation**: ≥ 14 d in the local vivarium before any intervention — enough for the microbiome to re-equilibrate to local feed / water / housing.
- **Cohousing**: LGG and control arms in **separate cages** with sentinel monitoring; ideally separate ventilated racks. Coprophagy across cages collapses the LGG-vs-control contrast — see [[../failure-modes/coprophagy-microbiome-equilibration]].
