# Caco-2 (human colorectal carcinoma → intestinal-epithelial-cell model)

Caco-2 monolayers on Transwell inserts are the in vitro workhorse for tight-junction biology — TEER, FD-4 flux, claudin localisation under cytokine challenge. Several mechanism-side papers in our corpus (Yan/Polk follow-ups, Domínguez-Díaz 2023, Hu 2024) use Caco-2 as the in vitro complement to in vivo work.

## What Caco-2 won't tell you

- Real microbiota interactions (no ecology, no commensal context).
- Mucus layer (Caco-2 lacks goblet-cell phenotype unless co-cultured with HT29-MTX).
- Strain colonisation kinetics in a complex community.

For this hypothesis (in vivo FD-4 readout) Caco-2 work is supportive mechanism evidence only.
