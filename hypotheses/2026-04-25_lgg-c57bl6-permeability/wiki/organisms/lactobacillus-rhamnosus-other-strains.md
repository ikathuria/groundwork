# *Lactobacillus rhamnosus* — non-GG strains

Other *L. rhamnosus* strains appear scattered through our corpus in two contexts:

1. **As comparators** to LGG within the same study (less common in our papers).
2. **As substitutes** in studies that simply say "Lactobacillus rhamnosus" without strain identification — these should be treated as **weak evidence for LGG specifically**.

## Strain-conservation evidence

- Bäuerl 2010 showed orthologous p40/p75 from *L. casei* BL23 (sister species) have similar barrier-protective activity — suggesting the soluble-protein mechanism is at least genus-conserved.
- The 2014 Singh et al. retraction (LGG fermented milk in aging mice) used "Lactobacillus rhamnosus" without strain ID — part of why it was retracted.

## What to do for this hypothesis

The hypothesis pre-registers **LGG / ATCC 53103 specifically**. Cross-strain papers in this wiki are useful background but **must not be cited as direct evidence for the hypothesis**.
