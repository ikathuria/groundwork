# Ussing chamber & transepithelial electrical resistance (TEER)

Ex vivo electrophysiological measurement of mucosal barrier integrity. Freshly excised intestinal tissue (or a polarised cell-monolayer) is mounted between two half-chambers; resistance to applied current measures predominantly tight-junction-restricted ion flow. Lower TEER ⇒ leakier barrier.

## Why pair it with FITC-dextran

- **Mechanism vs phenotype**: FD-4 reports the in vivo whole-animal flux; TEER reports the underlying mucosal conductance on the explant. They usually agree on direction; when they disagree, the in vivo flux is also influenced by gastric emptying, motility, and luminal volume — TEER isolates the epithelial component.
- **Region resolution**: TEER lets you compare ileum vs proximal colon vs distal colon on the same animal; FD-4 is whole-gut.

## Canonical parameters

| Parameter | Typical |
|---|---|
| Tissue | freshly stripped mouse ileum or proximal colon, 0.1 cm² aperture |
| Buffer | Krebs-Ringer, oxygenated, 37 °C |
| Endpoint | baseline TEER (Ω·cm²) and probe flux (FD-4, mannitol) over 2 h |
