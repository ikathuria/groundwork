# High-fat diet (HFD) gut-barrier model

Mice fed 45–60 % kcal-from-fat purified diets for 8–16 weeks develop diet-induced obesity, metabolic endotoxaemia, and a measurable, slow-onset increase in intestinal permeability. The canonical pairing is **Research Diets D12492 (60 % kcal fat)** vs the matched D12450J low-fat control.

## Relevance to this hypothesis

A second challenge model (after DSS and alcohol) in which LGG has been shown to attenuate barrier dysfunction (Arellano-García 2023/2025, Pan 2024, Wang 2025 MASLD/MASH). Its kinetics are slow (weeks) and the effect size is subtler than DSS — closer to the magnitude one would expect under a 30 % reduction threshold in unstressed mice.

If Pass 3 chooses a challenge variant, **HFD is the closest in physiology to a "naturally permeability-shifted" baseline** — the hyperpermeability is mild, chronic, microbiome-driven, and clinically translatable, unlike the brutal acute injury of DSS.

## Canonical parameters

| Parameter | Typical |
|---|---|
| Diet | Research Diets D12492 (60 % kcal fat) or D12451 (45 %) |
| Duration | 8–16 weeks |
| Endpoint | body weight, glucose tolerance, plasma LBP / LPS, FD-4, hepatic triglyceride |
