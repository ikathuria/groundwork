# Ex vivo intestinal permeability assay (X-IPA / Ussing-style)

Fresh mucosal explant assays in which a defined area of intestine is mounted between donor (luminal) and acceptor (serosal) chambers, FD-4 is added apically, and acceptor fluorescence is read across hours. Aldars-García et al. 2023 (in our protocols corpus) standardise this as "X-IPA".

## Why we'd add it

X-IPA dissects what FD-4 in vivo cannot:

- **Region resolution**: per-segment ileum vs proximal vs distal colon on the same animal.
- **Dynamics**: real-time accumulation curves rather than a single 4-h plasma timepoint.
- **Mechanism overlay**: the same mounted tissue can be probed pharmacologically (e.g. claudin-2 inhibitors) to test specific pathways.

## When to skip it

For a binary "does LGG lower in vivo permeability by 30 %?" answer, X-IPA is overkill. It belongs in a **mechanism follow-up** experiment after the in vivo readout is positive.
