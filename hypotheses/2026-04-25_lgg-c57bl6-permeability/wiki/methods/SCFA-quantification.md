# Short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) quantification

GC-FID or HPLC measurement of acetate, propionate, butyrate (the canonical trio) and the C5 / C6 branched-chain SCFAs in caecal content, faeces, or portal-vein plasma.

## Why this is mechanistically important

Multiple sources link the LGG-barrier effect to SCFA production — directly (LGG produces lactate and acetate, cross-fed to butyrate-producers) or indirectly (LGG colonisation shifts community composition toward butyrogens). Butyrate is the preferred energy substrate of colonocytes and stabilises HIF-1α to maintain barrier function.

## Canonical parameters

- Sample: caecal content, ~50 mg, snap-frozen at sacrifice, processed within 30 d.
- Internal standard: 2-ethylbutyric acid.
- Column: ZB-FFAP or similar polar capillary GC.
- Reporting: µmol/g caecal content (preferred over % composition).
