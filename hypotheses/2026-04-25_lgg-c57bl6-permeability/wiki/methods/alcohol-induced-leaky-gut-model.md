# Alcohol / ethanol-induced gut barrier disruption

Acute or chronic ethanol exposure used to model alcoholic liver disease and the gut hyperpermeability that precedes it. Two dominant variants in the corpus:

- **Lieber-DeCarli liquid diet** — chronic; ethanol replaces a fraction of dietary calories.
- **NIAAA chronic-plus-binge** (Bertola, Wang, Gao) — 10 d Lieber-DeCarli followed by a single oral binge; the canonical mouse model for alcoholic liver injury, with reproducible gut barrier loss at 9 h post-binge.

## Relevance to this hypothesis

Like DSS, this is a **challenge model**, not the unchallenged-baseline design our hypothesis targets. Multiple LGG papers in our corpus (Salemi 2023, Mishra 2023, Yang 2025, Pan 2024, Zhang 2026) report that LGG (live or supernatant or EPS) **mitigates ethanol-induced FD-4 elevation and tight-junction disassembly**. None demonstrates a comparable effect in unstressed mice. See [[../failure-modes/challenge-model-vs-baseline-mismatch]].

## Canonical parameters

- **Lieber-DeCarli**: 4–6 weeks, 4 % v/v ethanol in liquid diet, pair-fed isocaloric controls.
- **NIAAA**: 10 d 5 % ethanol in liquid diet → single 5 g/kg oral gavage on day 11; sacrifice 9 h later.
- **Acute binge alone**: 6 g/kg ethanol p.o.; barrier loss measurable at 4 h.
