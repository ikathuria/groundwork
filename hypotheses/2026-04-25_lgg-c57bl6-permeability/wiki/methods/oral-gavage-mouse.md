# Oral gavage in mice

Direct intragastric delivery of a defined volume / dose using a curved, ball-tipped feeding needle. The standard route for both the **probiotic intervention** (LGG suspension) and the **assay reagent** (FITC-dextran) in this hypothesis.

## Why we use it (vs drinking-water dosing)

- **Dose certainty**: drinking-water dosing depends on water intake — variable across mice and across cages, and probiotic viability decays over hours in water bottles at cage temperature.
- **Synchrony**: every animal is dosed within minutes of every other, which matters for time-point readouts (FD-4 plasma at 4 h post-gavage).
- **Single-strain**: avoids cross-contamination of control cages via shared water lines.

## Canonical parameters (mouse)

| Parameter | Typical | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Needle | 22G, 1.5", curved, stainless ball-tip (e.g. Kent Scientific FNS-22-1.5) | size up to 18G for chronic studies |
| Volume | 100–200 µL (≤ 10 mL/kg per IACUC norms) | larger volumes risk reflux & aspiration |
| Restraint | gentle scruff, head-tilt, immobile tail | training is rate-limiting; first 2 weeks of any LGG study should be under a senior tech |
| Frequency | once daily, same hour | LGG colonisation is transient; daily dosing required to maintain CFU |

## LGG-specific considerations

- **Prepare the dose on the day**, not the day before. Frozen LGG glycerol stock → MRS broth → log-phase growth → wash in PBS → resuspend at 5×10⁹ CFU/mL → 200 µL = 1×10⁹ CFU/mouse.
- **Confirm CFU on the dose-day plate**: serial-dilute the dosing suspension and plate on MRS agar at the time of gavage. Without this, viability loss between thaw and dosing is invisible. See [[../failure-modes/LGG-viability-loss-during-gavage-prep]].
- **Cold chain**: keep the gavage syringe-pool on ice; warm individual aliquots only at the moment of dosing. Room-temperature LGG suspensions lose ~0.5–1 log of viability per hour.

## FD-4 gavage specifics

- Fast 6 h prior; water ad lib. See [[../failure-modes/incomplete-fasting-before-gavage]].
- Wrap dosing tubes in foil to prevent FITC photobleaching. See [[../failure-modes/FITC-dextran-photobleaching]].
