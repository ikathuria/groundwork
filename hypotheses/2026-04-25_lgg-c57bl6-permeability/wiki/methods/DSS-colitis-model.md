# DSS-induced colitis (mouse)

Standard chemical model of acute / chronic colonic epithelial injury. DSS is added to the drinking water (typically **2–3 % w/v for 5–7 days**) and disrupts the colonic epithelium by direct cytotoxicity to colonocytes plus mucin damage. Recovery, weight loss, disease activity index, and barrier breakdown are the standard readouts.

## Relevance to this hypothesis

The hypothesis is about **baseline** intestinal permeability in unchallenged C57BL/6 mice — but **the bulk of the LGG-barrier evidence in our corpus comes from DSS models, not unchallenged mice**. Pass 3 must decide whether to:

1. Stay strictly with the unchallenged design (and accept the floor-effect risk — see [[../failure-modes/baseline-permeability-too-low-detect-30pct]]), or
2. Add a mild DSS arm (1 % × 5 d) as a positive control to confirm the FD-4 assay is sensitive in this animal cohort, or
3. Re-frame the hypothesis around a barrier-perturbed mouse and use DSS as the primary stressor.

The cross-source pattern is unambiguous: **published LGG → barrier effects in DSS mice are robust** (Si 2025, Chen 2025, Suntornsaratoon 2024, Liu 2025); **published LGG → barrier effects in unstressed adult mice are sparse and underpowered**.

## Canonical parameters

| Parameter | Typical |
|---|---|
| DSS molecular weight | 36–50 kDa (potency depends strongly on this; report lot) |
| Concentration | 2–3 % w/v in autoclaved drinking water |
| Duration | 5–7 d for acute; cyclic 3-cycle protocol for chronic |
| Endpoint | day 7–10: body weight, DAI, colon length, FD-4, histology |

## Common pitfalls

- **DSS lot variability**: different MW distributions and supplier lots give substantially different disease severity. Pre-test each lot.
- **Strain susceptibility**: C57BL/6J is more DSS-susceptible than BALB/c.
- **Microbiome dependence**: DSS severity drops in germ-free mice and varies with vendor microbiome. See [[../failure-modes/vendor-of-origin-microbiome-baseline]].
