# LGG exopolysaccharide (EPS)

The polysaccharide capsule layer LGG secretes. Two operationally distinct fractions:
- **Long galactose-rich EPS** — covers / shields the LGG cell surface.
- **Cell-wall-anchored EPS** — implicated in adhesion to mucus.

In Li et al. 2023 (PMC9987055) LGG EPS attenuated *Salmonella typhimurium*-induced colitis with measurable rescue of tight-junction protein expression — a direct example of an LGG-derived polysaccharide doing barrier work without viable bacteria. Mechanistically attributed to TLR4 modulation and EPS-driven anti-inflammatory shifts.

## Why this matters for the hypothesis

EPS is a strong candidate for the **"heat-killed LGG works as well as live LGG"** observation: heat inactivation does not destroy polysaccharide structure, so EPS survives intact in postbiotic preparations.
