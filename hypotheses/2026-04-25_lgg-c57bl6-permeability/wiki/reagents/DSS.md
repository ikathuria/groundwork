# Dextran sulfate sodium (DSS)

Sulfated polysaccharide (sulfate density ~17 %, MW typically 36–50 kDa for mouse colitis work). Dosed in drinking water at 2–3 % w/v for 5–7 d; disrupts the colonic epithelial barrier by direct cytotoxicity to colonocytes and mucin damage. Used in our corpus as the dominant **challenge model** in which LGG-barrier effects are demonstrated.

## Critical sourcing notes

- **Molecular weight matters**: 5 kDa DSS is non-colitogenic; 500 kDa DSS is much milder than 36–50 kDa. Always specify and report.
- **Lot-to-lot variability**: same supplier, different lots can give 30 % swings in disease severity. Pre-test each lot in pilot mice before the main study.
- **Vehicle**: autoclaved drinking water; replace daily (DSS solution degrades at room temperature).
