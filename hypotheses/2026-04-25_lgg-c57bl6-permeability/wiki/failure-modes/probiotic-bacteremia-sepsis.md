# Probiotic bacteraemia / sepsis (rare but serious)

## What it is

Live LGG can translocate from the gut lumen to the bloodstream in hosts whose barrier is severely compromised, whose immune defence is reduced, or both. This is a documented clinical adverse event in:

- Preterm neonates (Farella et al. 2025, [[../sources/2025-farella-lactobacillus-rhamnosus-sepsis-preterm-infant]] — sepsis case in a preterm infant after probiotic administration)
- Immunocompromised oncology patients
- Patients with central venous catheters
- Patients with major gut barrier breach (severe IBD, post-surgical, mucositis)

## Relevance to this hypothesis

- The intervention is in **healthy adult immunocompetent C57BL/6**. Sepsis risk is very low in this population.
- However: any animal that develops unexpected illness (lethargy, weight loss > 20 %, ruffled coat) **must** be excluded and considered for blood culture.
- IACUC review should explicitly note the LGG sepsis literature and require euthanasia criteria.

## Detection

- **Daily welfare scoring** (body weight, posture, coat, activity) for the full 28 d.
- Blood culture on any animal meeting humane-endpoint criteria, before euthanasia.
- Post-mortem: if any animal dies unexpectedly, send blood + spleen + liver for culture before discarding.

## Mitigation

1. Use immunocompetent adult animals only — exclude immunocompromised lines from this protocol.
2. No intervention in animals with concurrent injury / surgery.
3. Pre-defined humane endpoint criteria in IACUC protocol.
4. Adverse-event reporting plan.
