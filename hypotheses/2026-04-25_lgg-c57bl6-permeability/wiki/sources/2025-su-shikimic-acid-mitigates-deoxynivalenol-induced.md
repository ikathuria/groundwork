## Abstract

Deoxynivalenol (DON), a mycotoxin from Fusarium that contaminates cereals, can also induce intestinal injury. However, the mechanisms underlying DON-induced jejunal barrier injury remain unclear. This study demonstrates that shikimic acid (SA) alleviates DON-induced jejunal barrier damage and dysbiosis via antioxidant pathways. Fifty 5-week-aged male KM mice were divided into control (CON), model (MOD, 2.4 mg/kg bw DON), and SA-treated groups (LDG/MDG/HDG: 25/50/100 mg/kg bw SA + DON). After SA treatment, notably MDG, reversed DON-induced weight loss and jejunal hyperemia; ameliorated villus atrophy, crypt deepening and goblet cell loss, increasing villus/crypt ratio; reduced gut permeability markers (D-LA/DAO) and pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α/IL-6/IL-1β); and dose-dependently upregulated tight junction proteins (ZO-1/Occludin/Claudin1).
