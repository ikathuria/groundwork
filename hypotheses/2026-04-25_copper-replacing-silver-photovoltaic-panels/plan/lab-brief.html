Why The Route Is Copper Paste First

The wiki supports two broad routes: plated Ni/Cu stacks and copper paste. Plan-v1 chooses copper paste because it is closer to standard screen printing and avoids the current plating-chemistry procurement gap.

Silver Baseline

Commercial silver paste gives the control lot: mature, solderable, and costly.

Control arm

Copper Paste

Print copper paste in matched geometry, then prove it does not lose fill factor or contaminate equipment.

Selected route

Module Gate

Encapsulate mini-modules and stress them under damp heat before claiming panel relevance.

Reliability gate

Decision Logic

Copper only wins if it clears three gates: initial electrical parity, no copper migration above control baseline, and no worse damp-heat degradation than silver controls.

Performance gate

No more than 0.3 percentage point absolute efficiency loss versus silver.

Migration gate

SIMS/EDS sentinel samples must show no copper in silicon above controls.

Reliability gate

Damp-heat mini-modules must preserve power, fill factor, and EL behavior relative to controls.
