# Front Side Copper Metallization Paste for Silicon Solar Cells

## Summary

This NREL CRADA report addresses a practical manufacturing concern: whether copper metallization paste contaminates shared R&D or production equipment and harms subsequently fired silver-metallized cells. It is especially relevant to a near-term experiment because it treats implementation risk, not just device physics.

## Key Claims

- Adoption and testing of copper paste are hindered by concern that copper will contaminate equipment.
- The project objective was to test whether copper from pastes contaminates or degrades silver-metallized cells produced in the same firing furnace.
- The report is directly relevant to lab workflow design, furnace segregation, and decontamination assumptions.
- It should be treated as a process-compatibility source for copper paste routes.
