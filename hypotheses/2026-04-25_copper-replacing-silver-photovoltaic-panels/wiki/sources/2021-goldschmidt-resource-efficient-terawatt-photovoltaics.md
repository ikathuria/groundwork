# Technological learning for resource efficient terawatt scale photovoltaics

## Summary

Goldschmidt et al. provide systems-level context for material intensity in terawatt-scale photovoltaics. For this hypothesis, the paper is a scaling source: it explains why reducing [[../reagents/silver|silver]] intensity matters even if cell-level copper substitution requires additional reliability engineering.

## Key Claims

- Terawatt PV deployment makes material intensity a design constraint, not just a cost detail.
- Learning curves and technology shifts can reduce material demand per watt, but scaling can still increase absolute material pressure.
- Silver use in cell metallization is one of the major non-silicon resource questions for crystalline silicon PV.
- Copper-containing metallization is one plausible route, but must be integrated into a wider manufacturing and reliability framework.
