# International Technology Roadmap for Photovoltaic 2024

## Summary

The ITRPV roadmap gives industry context for silver reduction, copper-containing metallization, and interconnection materials. It is not a process protocol, but it is important for setting whether a copper-for-silver hypothesis is commercially relevant.

## Key Claims

- Metallization pastes containing silver and aluminum remain among the most process-critical and expensive non-silicon materials in crystalline silicon cell manufacturing.
- The roadmap expects continued silver reduction at the cell level and considers silver replacement with copper.
- Copper-containing metallization is expected to gain share especially in SHJ contexts, while silver remains the dominant front metallization material.
- Plating is still not a significant mass-production market share, despite continued interest.
- Copper wires are already expected to dominate module interconnection material market share.

## Supports / Contradicts

- Supports [[2021-goldschmidt-resource-efficient-terawatt-photovoltaics]] on silver scaling pressure.
- Provides a conservative market counterweight to highly positive lab demonstrations: copper-containing approaches are emerging, but silver is still expected to remain dominant.

## Notable Evidence

- The roadmap says silver remains cost critical due to world-market dependency.
- It distinguishes cell metallization from module interconnection, where copper wires are already common.
