# Progress in crystalline silicon heterojunction solar cells

## Summary

Liang et al. review crystalline silicon heterojunction technology, including metallization constraints and copper-related approaches. The source is useful for connecting copper substitution to SHJ-specific needs, where low-temperature processing and transparent conductive oxides affect feasible contact schemes.

## Key Claims

- SHJ cells are high-efficiency silicon devices but have metallization and low-temperature process constraints.
- Copper plating and copper-containing metallization are among routes explored to reduce silver use.
- Transparent conductive oxide quality, passivation, contact resistance, and metallization compatibility interact strongly in SHJ devices.
- Copper routes for SHJ must satisfy both electrical performance and low-temperature, reliability-compatible processing.
