# Barrier Properties of Electroplating Nickel Layer for Copper Metallization in Silicon Solar Cells

## Summary

Cheng et al. experimentally test electroplated nickel as a copper diffusion barrier on textured silicon. The study uses electroless nickel, annealing to form nickel silicide, then electroplated nickel and copper stacks, making it directly relevant to [[../methods/nickel-diffusion-barrier-plating|nickel barrier design]] for copper-for-silver photovoltaic metallization.

## Key Claims

- Electroless nickel can create a seed layer on textured silicon before nickel and copper electroplating.
- The authors annealed nickel at 500 deg C in Ar/H2 for 10 min to form nickel silicide before further plating.
- A Cu/Ni/ta-Si stack was built with approximately 1.5 um copper and nickel layers controlled by plating time.
- Cu3Si appears after annealing when copper penetrates the barrier; increasing nickel plating time from 60 to 120 s improves barrier behavior.
- The paper reports that a thin electroplated nickel layer can act as a diffusion barrier around 400 deg C, but Cu3Si formation still defines a failure boundary.
