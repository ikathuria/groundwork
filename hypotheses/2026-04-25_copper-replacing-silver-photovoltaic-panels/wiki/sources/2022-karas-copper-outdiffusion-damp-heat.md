# Copper Outdiffusion from Copper-Plated Solar Cell Contacts During Damp Heat Exposure

## Summary

Karas et al. are the corpus's strongest failure-archaeology source. They analyze encapsulated copper-plated cells after extended damp heat and find copper outdiffusion through tin and silver capping layers, plus evidence of copper at the silicon surface and in the cell bulk.

## Key Claims

- Copper-plated contacts are attractive alternatives to screen-printed silver but have unresolved long-term integrity questions.
- EDS evidence showed copper moving upward through tin and silver capping layers after damp-heat exposure.
- SIMS detected elevated copper at the silicon surface and in the silicon bulk in several damp-heat-degraded cells.
- Extended damp heat at 85 deg C / 85% RH is a critical stress for copper-plated contact stacks.
- Module packaging chemistry and encapsulant byproducts may help drive copper outdiffusion.
