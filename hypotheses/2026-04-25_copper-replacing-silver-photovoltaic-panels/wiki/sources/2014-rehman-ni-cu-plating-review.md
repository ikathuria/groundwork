# Review of the Potential of the Ni/Cu Plating Technique for Crystalline Silicon Solar Cells

## Summary

This review positions [[../methods/nickel-copper-plated-silicon-metallization|Ni/Cu plating]] as a candidate replacement for conventional [[../reagents/silver-conductive-paste|silver paste]] screen printing in [[../organisms/crystalline-silicon-solar-cell|crystalline silicon solar cells]]. It emphasizes lower contact resistance, reduced silver use, and finer self-aligned metal lines, while also flagging the need for a nickel barrier, adhesion control, background plating control, and process stability.

## Key Claims

- Screen-printed silver is commercially dominant but is limited by paste cost, contact resistance, and linewidth/aspect-ratio constraints.
- A typical Ni/Cu plated stack deposits nickel first, then copper as the main conductor, usually with a tin or silver capping layer.
- Nickel can both promote adhesion and serve as a diffusion barrier between copper and silicon.
- Light-induced plating can help plate solar-cell contacts while the device operates near short-circuit conditions.
- The main implementation risks are [[../failure-modes/copper-diffusion-into-silicon|copper diffusion]], [[../failure-modes/contact-adhesion-loss|adhesion loss]], [[../failure-modes/background-plating|background plating]], and process reproducibility.

## Supports / Contradicts

- Supports [[2018-cheng-nickel-barrier-copper-metallization]] on the need for a nickel barrier.
- Supports [[2024-itrpv-roadmap]] and [[2021-goldschmidt-resource-efficient-terawatt-photovoltaics]] on silver cost and supply pressure.
- Provides a more optimistic process outlook than [[2022-karas-copper-outdiffusion-damp-heat]], which documents copper mobility after damp-heat exposure.

## Notable Evidence

- The review states that copper creates recombination centers in silicon, so a barrier is required.
- Page 1 frames Ni/Cu plating as a path to lower silver use without relying solely on screen printing.
