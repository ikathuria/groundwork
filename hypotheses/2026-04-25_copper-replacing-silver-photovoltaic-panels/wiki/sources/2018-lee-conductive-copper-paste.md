# Conductive Copper Paste for Crystalline Silicon Solar Cells

## Summary

This open-access chapter reviews conductive copper pastes for [[../organisms/crystalline-silicon-solar-cell|crystalline silicon solar cells]]. It is useful for Pass 3 because it connects paste formulation and screen printing to the broader silver-replacement goal, rather than only considering electroplated copper.

## Key Claims

- Copper paste is a distinct route from plated copper and may be more compatible with existing screen-printing workflows.
- Copper particle oxidation, sintering chemistry, and contact formation are core formulation problems.
- Compared with silver paste, copper paste must solve both conductivity and stability while preventing copper from degrading silicon junction behavior.
- Paste-based routes can reduce process disruption but may face contact-resistance and firing-window constraints.
