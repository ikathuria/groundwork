# Aging tests of mini-modules with copper-plated heterojunction solar cells and pattern-transfer-printing of copper paste

## Summary

Lachowicz et al. test mini-modules made with copper-plated [[../organisms/silicon-heterojunction-solar-cell|silicon heterojunction cells]] and copper-paste pattern-transfer printing. The paper is important because it moves beyond cell-level metallization and into mini-module aging behavior.

## Key Claims

- Replacing silver with copper is framed as a key step for large-scale PV cost and material reduction.
- The work evaluates copper-plated heterojunction mini-modules and pattern-transfer-printed copper paste under aging tests.
- The study is directly relevant to module-level validation, not only cell contact fabrication.
- It highlights that silver reduction must preserve mini-module performance after damp heat and thermal cycling.
