# Copper Oxidation

## What It Is

Copper oxidation degrades conductor quality and sintering/contact behavior in copper pastes and exposed copper contacts.

## How It Manifests

- Higher line resistance or contact resistance.
- Poor sintering or incomplete conductive network formation.
- Instability after thermal or humid exposure.

## How To Detect It

- Four-point or transfer-length resistance measurements.
- SEM/EDS or XPS on fired paste contacts.
- Before/after I-V comparison after humidity and thermal stress.

## Mitigation

- Use protected copper particles or supplier-qualified copper paste.
- Control drying and firing atmosphere.
- Minimize air/moisture exposure per supplier instructions.
