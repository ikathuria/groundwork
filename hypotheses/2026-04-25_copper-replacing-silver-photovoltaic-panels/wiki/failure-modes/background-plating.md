# Background Plating

## What It Is

Background plating is unwanted metal deposition outside intended grid or contact regions. It can increase shading, create shunts, or contaminate surfaces.

## How It Manifests

- Metal deposits outside patterned openings.
- Reduced shunt resistance or localized shorts.
- Optical shading or poor appearance.

## How To Detect It

- Optical microscopy after plating.
- EL/PL imaging for shunts or recombination sites.
- Sheet resistance and shunt-resistance checks.

## Mitigation

- Improve pattern masking and surface cleaning.
- Control bath chemistry, current density, illumination, and plating time.
- Reject cells with visible off-pattern metal before module assembly.
