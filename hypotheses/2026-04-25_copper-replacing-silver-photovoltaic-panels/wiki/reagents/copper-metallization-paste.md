# Copper Metallization Paste

## Properties

Copper metallization paste is a printable conductor candidate for reducing silver usage in silicon solar cells. The main appeal is workflow compatibility with screen printing; the main risks are oxidation, contact quality, silicon contamination, and firing compatibility.

## Common Uses

- Rear-side or back-contact fingers and busbars.
- Fire-through copper paste under development for PERC-type cells.
- Pattern-transfer printed copper features in mini-module experiments.

## Alternatives

- [[silver-conductive-paste]]
- Plated [[copper]] with [[nickel]] barrier.
- Silver-coated copper paste particles.

## Gotchas

- Copper paste can require barriers or residual silver contact patterns to avoid direct Cu-Si risk.
- Shared furnace contamination must be explicitly controlled.
- Long-term damp-heat behavior may depend on encapsulant and stack chemistry.
