# Nickel/Copper Plated Silicon Metallization

## What It Measures / Does

This method replaces a screen-printed silver conductor with a plated contact stack, usually involving a nickel seed or barrier layer followed by copper as the main conductor. It targets lower metallization cost, lower silver demand, and potentially finer contact geometry.

## When To Use It

Use this route when the experiment needs a direct copper conductor stack on crystalline silicon or silicon heterojunction cells and has access to wet-chemical plating, patterned openings, and post-plating reliability tests. It is more process-intensive than copper paste but may reduce more silver if the barrier and capping stack work.

## Key Parameters

- Nickel seed/barrier deposition: electroless, light-induced electroless, or electroplated nickel.
- Copper conductor deposition: electroplated copper after the surface is conductive and patterned.
- Capping layer: often tin or silver to protect copper and support soldering/interconnection.
- Reliability screen: damp heat, thermal cycling, and contact-resistance tracking.

## Common Variants

- [[light-induced-plating|Light-induced plating]] for solar-cell contacts.
- Ni/Cu/Sn stacks.
- Ni/Cu/Ag stacks.
- Copper-plated SHJ mini-modules.
