# Copper Paste Screen Printing

## What It Measures / Does

Copper paste screen printing uses printable copper-containing paste as a lower-silver conductor in solar-cell metallization. It is closer to existing screen-printing workflows than wet plating but must solve oxidation, sintering, contact resistance, and contamination concerns.

## When To Use It

Use when the experimental plan needs an industrially familiar route and the laboratory can print, dry, fire, and electrically test cells or coupons. This route is attractive for quick comparison against silver paste controls.

## Key Parameters

- Paste formulation and copper particle protection.
- Finger width, height, and line resistance.
- Firing temperature / thermal profile.
- Whether silver fire-through contacts remain under copper features.
- Furnace contamination controls if copper and silver samples share equipment.

## Common Variants

- Rear-side copper fingers and busbars over silver point contacts.
- Pattern-transfer printed copper paste.
- Atmospheric fire-through copper paste with LECO post-treatment.
